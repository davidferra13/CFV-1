**Partner Profile: "Francis Mallmann" - Farm Dinner Co-host (Experiential Culinary)**

I am a chef focused on bringing the elemental spirit of food and fire to extraordinary settings. My work is less about the kitchen and more about the destination—transforming a meal into a collective, sensory experience. I collaborate with incredible producers and venues, turning what could be a simple dinner into a multi-layered, unforgettable narrative for my guests.

### Business Reality

Right now:
* I co-host, promote, or support **8 to 12** high-end, limited-attendance events, referrals, or partnerships per quarter.
* My contract structure is complex: typically, a **30% revenue share** on the net ticket sales (after venue commission and tax), plus a **$5,000 retainer** per successful, multi-day booking.
* My collaborations involve high-end, non-traditional venues (e.g., working farms, historical estates, private vineyards), requiring joint promotional efforts with the venue's concierge team, the farm's agricultural sales team, and my own brand marketing.
* The shared event location, calendar, and guest list are constantly fluctuating, often requiring coordination across three separate entity calendars (Farm, Venue, Me) and are subject to immediate environmental or seasonal availability constraints.
* Current coordination setup is a chaotic mix of dedicated email threads (one for ticketing, one for farm logistics, one for menu ideas), shared Google Docs that are never updated, and mandatory phone calls to resolve scheduling conflicts. There is absolutely no single source of truth.

---

### Primary Failure: Fragmented Guest Journey and Upsell Opportunity

My biggest operational pain point is the complete disconnect between the initial booking and the final guest experience, which kills crucial revenue streams. When a guest books through the venue's primary ticketing system, the farm's specific dietary needs (e.g., allergies, ethical sourcing requirements) are often lost in translation or require manual entry by a coordinator. This forces me to rely on scattered email confirmations to manage dietary restrictions or special requests, leading to last-minute ingredient sourcing nightmares or, worse, a poor guest experience. This operational failure costs me an estimated **$2,500 to $5,000** per event in lost upsell revenue—because I can't easily cross-sell the farm's premium product lines (like special aged cheeses or heirloom wines) to the guests who have already booked, nor can I track which ancillary services were purchased (e.g., wine pairing add-ons vs. pre-dinner cocktails).

---

### Structural Issue: Inventory and Menu Synchronization

The core problem is that the menu, the required ingredients, and the corresponding cost sheets are managed by three different people using three different versions of a spreadsheet. I might create a menu using a seasonal item from the farm, but the farm's logistics team only updates their inventory list weekly, and the venue's catering team only confirms what they can physically receive on the day. This results in constant, stressful back-and-forth emails debating if the item is available, if it can be transported, or if the initial cost estimate was accurate. I spend hours every week consolidating these disparate spreadsheets just to confirm the feasibility of the menu, wasting time that should be spent on creative development.

---

### Structural Issue: Referral and Booking Attribution

Tracking who brought who, and which specific promotion drove the booking, is a manual nightmare. When a guest is referred by the farm's sales contact, or if I run a joint promotion through the venue's website, the referral tracking is handled by different systems (a coupon code here, a mention in an email there). I have no single dashboard that shows, for a given event, how many attendees were booked via the farm partnership versus the venue's direct channel. This makes accurately calculating and justifying my **30% revenue share** extremely difficult, forcing me to spend time auditing spreadsheets instead of focusing on the next creative collaboration.

---

### Structural Issue: Collaborative Vendor Management and Compliance

Because my events happen in non-traditional, multi-jurisdictional spaces (a private farm, a municipal venue, etc.), the vendor management and compliance requirements are a logistical headache. The farm's setup requires me to track specialized equipment (e.g., specific wood types for open-fire cooking), and the venue requires me to submit temporary insurance riders and detailed staff manifests. I am constantly switching between the venue's vendor portal, the farm's operational checklist, and my own staff scheduling software. This fragmented approach means I often risk missing a critical compliance detail, which could jeopardize the entire event and lead to significant delays or outright cancellation.

---

### Structural Issue: Guest Communication and Booking Status

The guest journey lacks a centralized communication hub. The initial confirmation comes from the venue, the dietary details are updated via a separate email, the final itinerary is sent by the farm's coordinator, and then I have to send a final, exciting touch-up email. If a guest updates their booking status (e.g., changing from 2 to 3 people), they might update it in the venue's system, but the farm and my team might not see it immediately, leading to under-staffing or ingredient shortages. The lack of a single, real-time, authoritative source for the guest's *current* booking status and associated needs creates unnecessary stress and operational risk.

---

### Psychological Model

I optimize for: **Experience integrity and seamless flow.** Nothing can disrupt the narrative I am building with the food.
I refuse to compromise on: **The provenance and story of the ingredients.** If the system complicates the sourcing or fails to track the specific origin of a key ingredient, it's useless.
I avoid: **Administrative overhead that pulls me away from the creative process.** I will not use a tool that requires me to input data multiple times or spend more time on logistics than on the culinary vision.
I evaluate tools by: **How easily they integrate the physical location and the people who are there.** If it doesn't feel like it lives on site or connects directly to the operation, I won't bother.

I think about the guest experience first. Every piece of technology must serve to elevate the story, not complicate it. I am highly resistant to complexity and any system that forces me to become a data entry clerk.

---

### Pass / Fail Conditions

For this system to work for me, it must:

1. The system must **provide a single, real-time booking dashboard** that aggregates RSVPs from the venue, the farm, and my direct channels, showing the current, authoritative guest count and contact information.
2. The system must **allow the creation of a multi-stakeholder event itinerary** that maps specific required resources (equipment, staff, ingredients) from multiple partner locations (Farm A, Venue B) and track their availability simultaneously.
3. The system must **facilitate the structured capture and tracking of revenue share and promotion attribution** by linking specific ticket sales to the initial referring partner (e.g., the farm's sales contact) for accurate commission calculation.
4. The system must **allow for the dynamic creation and tracking of specialized menus and costing sheets** that can pull real-time ingredient inventory data from multiple, distinct vendor sources (e.g., the farm’s current yield reports).
5. The system must **manage complex, multi-tiered guest profiles** that track not just dietary restrictions, but also specific preferences, allergies, and ancillary purchase history (e.g., "Prefers red wine, allergic to nuts, purchased cheese pairing").
6. The system must **generate automatically updated, comprehensive operational checklists** for all involved parties (staff, vendors, partners) based on the final menu and guest manifest, ensuring compliance and resource readiness.
7. The system must **support the scheduling and management of multiple staff roles and vendors** across different physical locations on the same day, flagging potential resource conflicts or overlapping bookings instantly.
