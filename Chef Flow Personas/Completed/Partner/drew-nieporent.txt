**Partner Profile: "Drew Nieporent" - Venue Collaboration Partner (High-Profile Curator)**

I build experiences and connect brands with audiences. My job is to curate high-end, buzz-worthy events that generate media coverage and drive massive foot traffic. I don't just promote; I structure the entire narrative, from the initial concept pitch to the final post-event content rollout. My value is my network, my aesthetic, and my ability to guarantee press attention, which means I am deeply embedded in the marketing, promotion, and high-level contract negotiation phases of every collaboration.

### Business Reality

Right now:
* I co-host, promote, or support **3 to 5** events, referrals, or partnerships per **month**.
* We typically negotiate revenue share of **15% to 25%** of the gross ticket revenue, plus a guaranteed minimum sponsorship fee of **$5,000 - $10,000** per event, paid 30 days post-event.
* Our collaborations involve high-end venues (e.g., The EDITION, local gallery spaces), specific brand partnerships (e.g., high-end liquor brands, artisanal food producers), and joint promotional offers (e.g., "Show this post for 10% off").
* We have strict availability constraints: the event date, the capacity of the venue, and the required time for promotional material drop (which must be 6 weeks out).
* Our current coordination setup is a disaster: I manage initial pitches and contracts via **email**, final scheduling and guest lists are tracked on **Google Sheets** that are constantly edited, and immediate operational questions are answered via **WhatsApp** texts. There is no single source of truth for financials or guest flow.

---

### Primary Failure: Fragmented Revenue Tracking and Commission Calculation

My biggest operational problem is the sheer fragmentation of revenue streams. We have ticket sales, sponsorship payments, and separate vendor commissions, and they are all tracked in different ledgers. Because the payment schedule is complex (some upfront, some net 30, some tied to media performance), I spend an entire day every week reconciling these numbers. If a sponsor changes their payment terms by just 10%, I have to manually adjust spreadsheets, which introduces massive risk of error. This manual reconciliation process costs my team at least 10 hours per month, and if I miss a single data point, it can jeopardize future partnerships and damage trust with major sponsors.

---

### Structural Issue: Contractual Liability and Scope Creep

When I sign a partnership agreement, I am signing off on a scope that often gets immediately corrupted by "small additions." For example, a liquor brand sponsor might initially fund the bar setup, but then unilaterally decide they also want to cover the event signage and the staff uniform rental. We have to chase down these scope creep requests and confirm the budget impact via a separate email thread, which forces me to delay the final contract signing because I can't confirm the total cost of goods sold (COGS) or the total liability.

---

### Structural Issue: Guest Handoff and Experience Continuity

The moment a guest arrives, the experience must be seamless, regardless of which partner is responsible for them. If I promote a cocktail tasting (Partner A) and the ticket flow requires them to purchase a meal (Partner B), the transition has to be instantaneous. Right now, we rely on printed schedules or verbal instructions, which means my team sometimes directs guests to the wrong location, or the partner responsible for the next step doesn't have the pre-sold guest list, resulting in lost sales and a poor first impression that reflects badly on my brand.

---

### Structural Issue: Vendor Compliance and Booking Coordination

I am coordinating dozens of external vendors—florists, sound engineers, caterers, experiential artists—all of whom are contracted through different channels. When a vendor needs to make a last-minute change (e.g., the floral arrangement needs to be moved 15 feet or the sound tech needs an extra power outlet), there is no central place for that operational change. I end up fielding calls and texts from the venue manager, the caterer, and the vendor, forcing me to become an operational project manager instead of a creative curator.

---

### Structural Issue: Real-Time Promotional Asset Tracking

My promotional assets are my lifeblood. I create high-value content (photos, videos, stories) that must be tied back to specific revenue streams. After an event, I need to know exactly how many ticket sales, how much bar revenue, and how many vendor sign-ups were directly attributable to the Instagram post I made on Tuesday versus the press release I sent on Thursday. Because the sales platform, the point-of-sale system, and the promotion tracking are completely separate, I have no accurate, real-time attribution data.

---

### Psychological Model

I optimize for: **Exclusivity and narrative control.** I need the tool to make me look like I am running the show, even if I'm just coordinating it.
I refuse to compromise on: **The aesthetic and the guest experience.** If the operational friction is visible to the guest, the partnership fails.
I avoid: **Spreadsheets and manual data entry.** If I have to copy/paste data more than twice, I lose my mind.
I evaluate tools by: **How quickly they consolidate complex data into actionable, high-level insights.**

I think in terms of *stories* and *impact*. Everything has to feed back into the narrative of the event. If a process is clunky, it breaks the story, and if the story breaks, the revenue dries up. I need a system that is beautiful, intuitive, and powerful enough to handle the scale of my ambition.

---

### Pass / Fail Conditions

For this system to work for me, it must:

1.  The system must **automatically calculate** and reconcile the total revenue split (ticket sales + sponsorship fees + vendor commissions) against the signed contract terms, generating a clear, auditable statement 48 hours after the event close.
2.  The system must **provide a central hub** for all contractual documents, scope creep requests, and necessary operational amendments, preventing reliance on email chains.
3.  The system must **allow me to map** specific promotional assets (e.g., "Instagram Post #3") to the resulting ticket sales, bar revenue, and on-site bookings to prove ROI to my sponsors.
4.  The system must **manage and coordinate** multi-vendor operational needs (power requirements, layout changes, staff assignments) in a single, visible timeline that all partners and vendors can access and update.
5.  The system must **handle the guest journey** by allowing the transfer of pre-sold guest lists and necessary check-in details between multiple partnering vendors (e.g., from the ticketing system to the catering system).
6.  The system must **store and categorize** all high-quality media assets (photos, videos, testimonials) directly linked to the specific event, partner, and revenue stream for future marketing pitches.
7.  The system must **support multiple currencies and payment schedules** within one platform to accurately model and forecast cash flow for complex, multi-partner ventures.
