**Client Profile: "Megan" - Group Event Coordinator for Sister's Bachelorette (Referral, Multi-Party Payment, Airbnb Rental)**

I'm Megan. I'm booking a private chef dinner for my younger sister's bachelorette weekend in Kennebunk, Maine. We're renting an Airbnb. The group is 6+ women from Connecticut, Rhode Island, and Massachusetts. Nobody's local.

I found the chef through a referral chain: Anthony recommended him to my cousin's husband, who passed it to me. I don't actually know Anthony personally, but the recommendation was strong enough that I went with it sight unseen.

Right now:
* I'm the sole point of contact but I'm not the decision-maker on the menu; my sister (the bride) is
* I'm relaying decisions between the chef and my sister via screenshots and texts
* The group needs to split payment, probably through Venmo, collected by one person (me)
* We've already received a 20-item menu and my sister has highlighted her picks
* Communication has been sporadic on both sides; life is busy, no hard feelings
* I need a total price so I can tell the girls what to Venmo me
* The event is at a rental property I've never been to before

### Primary Failure: I'm a Relay, Not a Decision-Maker

My biggest operational problem is that I'm the booking contact but my sister is making the actual choices. Every decision requires me to relay information back and forth. The chef sends me the menu, I forward it to my sister, she highlights what she wants, I screenshot it back to the chef. If there's a question about chocolate vs fruit dessert, the chef asks me, I ask her, I relay back.

This creates delays, potential miscommunication, and forces the chef to repeat himself because information gets lost in translation. I also can't answer questions on the spot during phone calls because I need to "check with my sister."

This relay problem means the chef can't get a fast answer on anything. Menu changes, plate decisions, surprise elements; everything has a 24-48 hour delay while I play telephone.

---

### Structural Issue: Group Payment Splitting with No System

There are 6+ women attending. We need to split the total evenly (or close to it). There's no invoice system; the chef said "Venmo." I need a total number so I can divide it and tell everyone what they owe. If the price changes because menu picks change, I have to re-communicate to everyone. There's no shared view of what we're paying for or what it costs.

I can't even tell the girls a ballpark right now because I haven't received the final total. This creates pressure on me as the organizer.

---

### Structural Issue: Logistics at a Rental Property

We're at an Airbnb. I don't know what's in the kitchen. The chef offered to bring his own plates or use ours. We had to negotiate this verbally on a phone call: apps and entrees on his plates, dessert on ours, he cleans up and leaves during dessert. This was a custom arrangement that won't be written down anywhere unless someone texts a summary.

If a detail gets forgotten (like "they only need dessert plates"), it creates day-of confusion.

---

### Structural Issue: Secret Surprise Element with No Paper Trail

The chef asked what would surprise my sister. I said chocolate. He's planning something. But my sister is also picking her own dessert from the menu. If she picks something chocolatey, there could be a collision. The chef said "don't worry, I'll figure it out," but there's no system tracking that this surprise exists, what it is, or how it adapts based on her final menu picks.

If the chef forgets this detail or loses the note, the surprise disappears.

---

### Structural Issue: Referral Trust Replaces Normal Business Process

Because this came through Anthony, the chef waived the deposit and offered flexible payment timing ("pay when you see me, or after, or whenever"). This is generous but creates ambiguity. When exactly do I pay? Before? After? The chef said "when we confirm the menu" or "when I see you guys." Those are different moments.

For a group splitting costs, I need a concrete "pay by X date" so I can collect from everyone in advance. "Whenever" doesn't work when 6 people need to coordinate.

---

### Structural Issue: Communication Channel Chaos

We've talked on the phone, texted, and the chef said screenshots are fine. But there's no single place where the confirmed menu, the price, the logistics (plates, timing, cleanup), and the surprise plan all live together. It's scattered across a phone call, future texts, and my sister's highlighted screenshot.

If either of us loses a text thread or forgets a verbal agreement, we're reconstructing from memory.

---

### Psychological Model

I optimize for:
- Ease of coordination: I need to relay info with minimal effort and maximum clarity
- Group fairness: Everyone pays their share, no one feels blindsided by costs
- Not being annoying: The chef said "don't worry about being annoying" but I still feel weird sending multiple texts
- My sister's happiness: This is her weekend; I want it to feel special and effortless for her

I refuse to compromise on:
- Clarity on price: I can't collect money from people without a number
- Written confirmation: Verbal agreements make me nervous even if the vibe is casual
- The surprise: If we're doing a secret chocolate thing, it needs to actually happen

I make decisions by:
- Checking with my sister first (she's the real client)
- Defaulting to "whatever's easier" when given options (plates, timing, payment)
- Trusting referrals over research (Anthony said go, so I went)

---

### Pass/Fail Conditions

1. **Relay Access**: System must allow me to share a menu/choices view with my sister directly, so she can pick without me screenshotting and forwarding
2. **Group Payment**: System must show a total, allow splitting, and give each person a way to pay their share (or at minimum, give me a number to distribute)
3. **Logistics Capture**: Plate arrangement, cleanup plan, and arrival details must be recorded somewhere both parties can reference
4. **Surprise Tracking**: Chef must have a way to note "secret chocolate element" tied to this event, visible only to him, that adapts if menu picks conflict
5. **Price Transparency**: I need the total before the event, with enough lead time to collect from 6 people
6. **Confirmation Summary**: After the phone call and text exchanges, there should be ONE place with the confirmed menu, price, logistics, and timeline
7. **Referral Context**: The system should note this came through Anthony so the chef remembers the trust level and deposit waiver without re-explaining
8. **No-Pressure Communication**: System should let me send updates (menu picks, questions) without feeling like I'm being "annoying" or interrupting
9. **Rental Property Mode**: System should prompt for relevant rental logistics (kitchen equipment, plates, cleanup expectations) when event location is not client's home
10. **Decision-Maker vs Contact**: System must distinguish between "who's booking" (me) and "who's choosing" (my sister) without making my sister create an account or jump through hoops
