Chef Profile: "Elijah Park" - Private Chef (Menu Versioner)

My menus are constantly evolving, but I don’t have version control. I tweak dishes, swap components, adjust plating - but I can’t track what changed, what worked, and what didn’t. I’m iterating blindly.

Business Reality
Right now:

9–14 events per month

$20,000–$45,000 monthly revenue

3–6 menu revisions per event before finalization

20–50 historical menu variants with no structured archive

Feedback stored in messages, not tied to specific versions

Frequent reuse of dishes without knowing their last “state”

Primary Failure: No Version History
I don’t know which version of a dish performed best. I change things based on memory or recent feedback, but I can’t compare iterations. My workaround is keeping separate docs, but they drift and duplicate.

Structural Issue: Feedback Not Linked to Versions
Client or guest feedback isn’t tied to the exact version served. I get “loved the dessert,” but which version? My workaround is guessing based on date.

Structural Issue: Component Drift
A dish’s components evolve independently - sauce changes, garnish changes - but I don’t track dependencies. My workaround is rewriting recipes, which loses lineage.

Structural Issue: Reuse Without Context
I reuse dishes across events, but I don’t know the last adjustments made. My workaround is rebuilding from scratch, wasting time.

Psychological Model
I optimize for: controlled iteration and continuous improvement
I refuse to compromise on: knowing what actually worked
I avoid: repeating past mistakes due to lack of history
I evaluate tools by: whether they give me clear lineage and comparisons

Pass / Fail Conditions
For this system to work for me, it must:

Version every menu and dish automatically with change tracking.

Attach feedback to specific versions and components.

Allow side-by-side comparison of iterations.

Track component dependencies within dishes.

Enable reuse of past versions with full context.

Maintain a clean, searchable history of all menus.

Surface best-performing versions based on outcomes.
