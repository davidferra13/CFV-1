Chef Profile: "Riley Thornton" - Private Chef (Timing Controller)

I don’t struggle with cooking - I struggle with timing everything perfectly. Courses drift, prep overlaps, and service pacing breaks. I need a system that treats time as strictly as ingredients.

Business Reality
Right now:

7–11 events per month

5–10 courses per event

2–4 timing slips per service (courses late or early)

30–60 prep tasks per event

Timing tracked mentally or with rough notes

Frequent last-minute adjustments during service

Primary Failure: No Precise Timing Model
I know how long things take individually, but not how they interact. Prep, rest, plating, service - all overlap. Without a structured timeline, I’m constantly reacting. My workaround is building rough timelines, but they break with any change.

Structural Issue: No Dependency Mapping
Tasks depend on each other (you can’t plate before resting, can’t reduce before prep). I don’t have a system mapping these dependencies. My workaround is experience, which fails under complexity.

Structural Issue: No Real-Time Adjustment
When something slips, I don’t have a system recalculating the rest of the service. My workaround is improvisation.

Structural Issue: No Pacing Feedback Loop
I don’t track how pacing actually went vs. planned. My workaround is memory.

Psychological Model
I optimize for: smooth, consistent service flow
I refuse to compromise on: proper pacing between courses
I avoid: reactive timing adjustments
I evaluate tools by: whether they make timing predictable and adjustable

Pass / Fail Conditions
For this system to work for me, it must:

Build a full event timeline with task dependencies.

Adjust the timeline dynamically when something changes.

Provide real-time cues during service.

Track actual vs. planned timing for improvement.

Integrate prep, cook, and service phases into one flow.

Allow quick adjustments without rebuilding the timeline.

Ensure consistent pacing across events.
