Client Profile: "Aaron Blake" - DevOps Engineer, Cloud Infrastructure (Uptime)

If the system isn’t reliable 24/7, I won’t use it. Pricing needs to be available anytime, anywhere.

Business Reality
Right now:

I book 4–6 private dinners per year, often at odd hours.

Budget ranges from $1,200 to $4,500.

I expect to configure and pay regardless of time or load.

Dietary needs are minimal but must persist.

Locations vary but should not affect availability.

I operate across time zones.

I expect systems to be resilient.

Primary Failure: Pricing Downtime
The biggest issue is when pricing isn’t available - errors, timeouts, or maintenance windows. If I can’t see a price, I can’t proceed. That’s immediate abandonment.

Structural Issue: Dependency Failures
Pricing relies on external services that fail - APIs, databases, etc. That creates cascading issues. Core pricing should not depend on fragile systems.

Structural Issue: No Fallback Mechanism
When something fails, there’s no fallback. The system just breaks. I expect degraded modes that still allow pricing and booking.

Structural Issue: Rate Limiting or Throttling
If I interact too quickly and get blocked, that’s unacceptable. Pricing should scale with usage.

Psychological Model
I optimize for: reliability and continuous availability
I refuse to compromise on: always-accessible pricing
I avoid: systems that fail under load or at odd times
I evaluate tools by: uptime and resilience

Pass / Fail Conditions
For this system to work for me, it must:

Provide pricing availability 24/7 with no downtime.

Minimize dependencies that can cause pricing failures.

Offer fallback pricing mechanisms if primary systems fail.

Handle high interaction rates without throttling.

Maintain consistent performance across time zones.

Ensure booking can proceed even in degraded conditions.

Deliver a resilient, fault-tolerant pricing system.
