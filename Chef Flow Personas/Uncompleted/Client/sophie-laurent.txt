Client Profile: "Sophie Laurent" - Senior QA Engineer, Payments Platforms (Breaker)

My job is to find where systems fail. If pricing breaks under any condition, I assume it’s unreliable everywhere.

Business Reality
Right now:

I book 3–6 private dinners per year, usually 2–8 guests.

Budget ranges from $1,000 to $5,000.

I test flows instinctively - I try edge cases without thinking.

Dietary needs vary per event and must be handled consistently.

Locations vary but should not introduce bugs.

I use both mobile and desktop to cross-check behavior.

I expect systems to handle edge cases gracefully.

Primary Failure: Edge Case Pricing Breaks
The biggest issue is when pricing fails under edge conditions - adding/removing guests rapidly, toggling options, or switching configurations. Totals desync, freeze, or display incorrect values. That’s a critical failure. If it breaks once, I don’t trust it at all.

Structural Issue: State Desynchronization
Different parts of the UI show different totals - cart vs checkout vs summary. That means state is not centralized. It’s a fundamental flaw.

Structural Issue: Invalid Combinations Allowed
I can select combinations that later get rejected or re-priced. That means validation is happening too late. Everything selectable should already be valid and priced.

Structural Issue: Regression Risk
Small changes cause pricing to behave inconsistently. That tells me the system isn’t robust. I expect stability across updates.

Psychological Model
I optimize for: correctness under all conditions
I refuse to compromise on: consistent, bug-free pricing
I avoid: systems that fail under stress or edge cases
I evaluate tools by: how hard it is to break them

Pass / Fail Conditions
For this system to work for me, it must:

Maintain a single source of truth for pricing across all views.

Handle rapid input changes without desynchronization or errors.

Prevent invalid configurations from being selectable.
