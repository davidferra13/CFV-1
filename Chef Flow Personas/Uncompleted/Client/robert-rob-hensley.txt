Client Profile: "Robert “Rob” Hensley" - Owner, Regional HVAC Company (Minimalist)

I’m not here to manage a process. I just want a good dinner, handled cleanly, without me learning a new system. If it feels like work, I’m out.

Business Reality
Right now:

I book 3–5 private dinners per year - anniversaries, small family gatherings, occasional client dinners. Usually 4–8 people.

Budget is straightforward: $1,200–$3,500. I don’t want surprises or line-item debates.

I expect to pay once, maybe twice (deposit + final). I’m not splitting payments or managing other people.

Guest details are simple: one gluten-free, one “no seafood,” that’s about it. I’ll tell you once.

Events are almost always at my house. Standard kitchen, nothing fancy. I assume a professional can figure it out.

I use text and email. That’s it. No apps, no logins, no dashboards.

My wife might weigh in on menu preferences, but I’m the one booking and paying.

Primary Failure: Too Much Process
The biggest issue is that booking a chef turns into a project. I send an inquiry, and suddenly I’m filling out forms, reviewing documents, confirming details across multiple messages. I don’t want to manage anything. I’ve walked away from bookings because it felt like I was doing half the work. I’m paying to not think about it - if I have to track details or repeat myself, the system is failing.

Structural Issue: Input Overload
Most systems ask for too much upfront - detailed questionnaires, preferences, timelines. I don’t have that ready, and I don’t want to spend time figuring it out. I’d rather give a few basics and let the chef handle the rest. When I’m forced to input everything myself, I either rush it or abandon it.

Structural Issue: Communication Drag
Back-and-forth messaging kills momentum. I don’t want a conversation - I want a result. If I ask for availability and pricing, I expect a clear answer, not a thread that drags on for hours or days. Every extra message increases the chance I lose interest or forget to respond.

Structural Issue: Payment Confusion
Payment should be the easiest part, but it rarely is. Invoices, links, different methods - it’s inconsistent. Sometimes I don’t even know if I’m fully booked until I ask. I want a simple, clear transaction that locks everything in. No ambiguity.

Psychological Model
I optimize for: simplicity and minimal time spent
I refuse to compromise on: clarity of price and confirmation
I avoid: forms, apps, and extended communication
I evaluate tools by: how quickly they get me from “I want this” to “it’s booked and handled”

Pass / Fail Conditions
For this system to work for me, it must:

Allow me to submit a booking request with minimal input (date, headcount, basic preferences) in under a minute.

Provide a clear, immediate response with availability, a simple menu direction, and total price.

Require no account creation or app download to complete the booking.

Offer a single, straightforward payment flow (deposit or full payment) that confirms the booking instantly.

Capture and retain the few details I provide without asking me to repeat them.

Minimize communication to only what’s necessary - no long threads or redundant questions.

Give me a simple confirmation with everything handled, so I don’t have to think about it again before the event.
