Client Profile: "Emily Dawson" - Elementary School Teacher (First-Timer)

I’ve never done anything like this before. This is my one big splurge - birthday, milestone, whatever you want to call it - and I want it to feel special. I have no idea how hiring a private chef works. I’m excited, but also kind of nervous that I’m going to mess something up or not know what to say.

Business Reality
Right now:

I’m planning 1 major private dinner, maybe once every few years. This is a one-time, meaningful event for 4–6 people.

My budget is around $800–$2,000. That’s already a stretch for me, so I need to feel confident in what I’m getting.

I expect to pay a deposit, but I don’t know how much is normal or when I should pay the rest.

My guests are close friends or family. Some are picky, one is vegetarian, and I don’t want anyone to feel left out.

The event is at my apartment. Small kitchen, limited space. I don’t know if that matters.

I don’t know what I’m supposed to provide - ingredients, plates, anything. I assume the chef handles it, but I’m not sure.

I use basic tools - text, email. I don’t use complex apps or systems.

Primary Failure: No Guidance
The biggest issue is that I don’t know what I’m doing, and the system assumes I do. I don’t know what to ask, what decisions matter, or what’s normal. If I’m just given options without explanation, I feel overwhelmed. I don’t want to guess and get it wrong. I need to be guided step-by-step, or I start to doubt whether I should even be doing this.

Structural Issue: Decision Overload Without Context
I’m asked to choose things - menu style, courses, service type - but I don’t understand what those choices mean in practice. What’s the difference between 3 courses and 4? What actually happens during the dinner? Without context, every decision feels risky. I don’t want to feel like I’m building something blindly.

Structural Issue: Fear of Doing It Wrong
I’m worried about making mistakes - underbooking, picking the wrong menu, not having enough space or equipment. There’s no system reassuring me that “this works” or “you’re good.” That uncertainty makes the whole process stressful instead of exciting.

Structural Issue: Pricing Anxiety
This is a big expense for me. If I don’t understand the pricing clearly, I get nervous. I don’t want hidden costs or surprises. I need to see exactly what I’m paying for and feel like it’s worth it.

Psychological Model
I optimize for: feeling confident and taken care of
I refuse to compromise on: understanding what’s happening and not feeling lost
I avoid: making decisions without context or reassurance
I evaluate tools by: how much they guide me and reduce uncertainty

Pass / Fail Conditions
For this system to work for me, it must:

Provide a guided, step-by-step experience that explains each decision in simple, clear terms.

Offer examples or previews (menus, timelines, what the night looks like) so I understand what I’m booking.

Automatically validate my setup (guest count, kitchen, space) and reassure me that it will work.

Clearly explain what the chef handles versus what I need to provide.

Show transparent pricing upfront with no hidden fees or surprises.

Allow the chef to take the lead after initial input, guiding me instead of expecting me to manage everything.

Give me a clear, final confirmation that everything is handled so I can relax and enjoy the experience.
