Client Profile: "Evan Roth" - Principal Frontend Engineer, E-Commerce Platforms (Cart Purist)

I’ve spent years building carts, checkout flows, and pricing engines. There is no excuse for not showing a live total. If I can add items to a cart anywhere on the internet and see the number update instantly, I expect the same here. If I don’t see that, I assume the system is broken or hiding something.

Business Reality
Right now:

I book 6–8 private dining experiences per year, typically 2–6 guests. Occasionally up to 10 for group events.

Budget ranges from $1,000 to $4,500. I’m not trying to minimize cost - I’m trying to understand it instantly.

I expect to complete a booking in one continuous flow: configure → review → pay.

Dietary needs are lightweight but must be captured correctly - usually one restriction per event.

Locations are predictable - my apartment or a friend’s place. I expect location to be just another variable in the system.

I operate entirely through web/mobile UI. I do not want to message a human to complete a transaction.

I think in UI states. If something isn’t visible, it doesn’t exist.

Primary Failure: No Cart Mental Model
The biggest issue is that there’s no cart. I should be able to “add” a dinner configuration the same way I add items to any product cart. Instead, I’m forced into a fragmented flow where I configure something without seeing its cost, then get a quote later. That breaks the mental model completely. If I can’t see a running total, I don’t feel like I’m buying anything - I feel like I’m requesting something. I don’t request. I purchase.

Structural Issue: Missing Line Items
There’s no concept of line items. A dinner is treated as one opaque block instead of a composition of parts - courses, guests, add-ons, staffing, travel. I want to see each component as a line item with its own cost. Right now, I get a single number with no breakdown, which makes it impossible to understand or adjust.

Structural Issue: No Persistent State
If I navigate away or refresh, my configuration is gone or resets. That’s unacceptable. A cart should persist. I should be able to build a dinner, come back later, and see the same configuration with the same pricing. Without persistence, the system feels fragile and untrustworthy.

Structural Issue: UI Lag on Pricing
Even when pricing exists, it often lags or requires a manual refresh. That’s a failure. Pricing updates should be instantaneous - sub-100ms. If I change guest count and wait even a second for the total to update, it feels broken. That hesitation creates doubt.

Psychological Model
I optimize for: immediate visual feedback and continuous state
I refuse to compromise on: a live, persistent cart with accurate pricing
I avoid: any flow that separates configuration from cost visibility
I evaluate tools by: whether they behave like a modern e-commerce checkout

Pass / Fail Conditions
For this system to work for me, it must:

Provide a persistent cart where my dinner configuration lives and updates in real time.

Display a continuously updating total price as I add, remove, or modify components.

Break down pricing into clear line items (per guest, per course, add-ons, fees).

Update pricing instantly with no perceptible lag when inputs change.

Preserve my configuration and pricing state across sessions and devices.

Allow me to move directly from cart to payment without reconfiguration or revalidation.

Eliminate any “request quote” step - everything must function as a direct purchase flow.
