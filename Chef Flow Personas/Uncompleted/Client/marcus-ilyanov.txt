Client Profile: "Marcus Ilyanov" - Senior Software Engineer, Payments & Checkout Systems (Determinist)

I’ve built checkout systems. I know exactly what’s possible. If I can’t see a running total in real time as I configure something, I assume the system is either poorly designed or intentionally hiding something. Either way, I’m out.

Business Reality
Right now:

I book 4–7 private dining experiences per year, usually 2–8 guests. Sometimes it’s just me and my partner, sometimes small groups.

Budget ranges from $800 to $5,000 depending on the setup. I’m flexible, but I need to see the number evolve as I build.

I expect to pay immediately once I’ve configured what I want - no intermediate steps, no hidden recalculations.

Dietary needs are simple but precise - one allergy or preference that must be handled correctly.

Locations are standard apartments or short-term rentals. I expect constraints to be factored automatically.

I interact through web or mobile. I don’t want to talk to anyone just to complete a transaction.

I think in systems - inputs, outputs, deterministic behavior. If I can’t model it mentally, I don’t trust it.

Primary Failure: Non-Deterministic Pricing
The biggest failure is when pricing is not directly tied to inputs in real time. If I select a 4-course dinner and input 6 guests, I should immediately see the computed total. If I add a wine pairing or upgrade a dish, I should see the delta instantly. When pricing is delayed, hidden, or requires manual follow-up, the system breaks its contract. It’s no longer deterministic - it’s opaque. That’s unacceptable in any checkout flow.

Structural Issue: No Live Pricing Engine
Most systems treat configuration and pricing as separate steps. You “build” something, then request a quote. That’s fundamentally wrong. Pricing is part of the configuration. Every selectable option should have a defined cost or formula attached. If I can click it, I should see its financial impact immediately. Otherwise, it’s not a real system - it’s a placeholder.

Structural Issue: Hidden Multipliers
Guest count, course count, staffing, travel - these are all multipliers. But they’re rarely exposed. I want to see the formula: base price × guests, plus fixed fees, plus add-ons. Right now, I get a total number with no visibility into how it was derived. That makes it impossible to reason about changes or trust the output.

Structural Issue: Checkout Discontinuity
Even when pricing is shown, it often resets or changes at checkout. I’ve seen totals shift between “estimate” and “final invoice.” That’s a critical failure. The number I see while configuring should be the number I pay, within clearly defined bounds. Any discrepancy breaks trust immediately.

Psychological Model
I optimize for: determinism, transparency, and immediate feedback
I refuse to compromise on: real-time, input-driven pricing
I avoid: any system that separates configuration from cost
I evaluate tools by: whether I can predict the total before the system shows it

Pass / Fail Conditions
For this system to work for me, it must:

Display a live, continuously updating total price as I configure the service (course count, guest count, add-ons).

Tie every selectable option to a visible cost or formula so its impact is immediately clear.

Show the full pricing breakdown (base cost, per-person multiplier, fixed fees, add-ons) in real time.

Update totals instantly with zero latency when any input changes - no “recalculate” step.

Maintain price consistency from configuration through checkout, with no unexpected changes.

Allow me to complete the entire flow - from selection to payment - without requiring human interaction.

Eliminate any “request quote” or delayed pricing mechanisms entirely; everything must be computable on the spot.
