Client Profile: "Elena Petrova" - International Business Consultant (Multi-Currency)

I operate across countries constantly. If pricing isn’t clear in my currency and adaptable globally, I don’t engage.

Business Reality
Right now:

I book 5–10 private dining experiences per year across different countries.

Budgets range from €2,000 to €15,000 depending on location.

I expect pricing in local currency and my preferred currency simultaneously.

Payment methods vary by region.

Dietary needs vary by culture and must be respected.

Locations are international - homes, villas, hotels.

I expect systems to adapt globally without friction.

Primary Failure: Currency Ambiguity
If I see pricing in a currency I don’t use without conversion, I hesitate. I need immediate clarity. I shouldn’t have to calculate exchange rates.

Structural Issue: No Multi-Currency Support
Systems often lock into one currency. That’s not acceptable. I need flexibility and transparency across currencies.

Structural Issue: Payment Method Mismatch
Different regions prefer different payment methods. If my preferred method isn’t supported, I won’t proceed.

Structural Issue: Hidden Conversion Fees
If conversion fees aren’t visible, I assume the worst. Transparency is mandatory.

Psychological Model
I optimize for: global clarity and adaptability
I refuse to compromise on: clear currency representation
I avoid: systems that feel region-locked
I evaluate tools by: how well they handle international use

Pass / Fail Conditions
For this system to work for me, it must:

Display pricing in both local and preferred currencies simultaneously.

Update conversions in real time.

Show any fees or exchange costs clearly.

Support region-appropriate payment methods.

Maintain consistent pricing logic across currencies.

Allow seamless booking regardless of location.

Eliminate ambiguity in international transactions.
