Client Profile: "Victor Salgado" - Corporate Lawyer (Compliance-Oriented)

If pricing isn’t clear, consistent, and documented, it creates risk. I need defensible numbers.

Business Reality
Right now:

I book 4–7 private dining events per year for clients and internal teams.

Budgets range from $2,000 to $10,000.

I require invoices that match quoted pricing exactly.

Dietary needs are secondary but must be handled.

Locations vary but must be documented.

I often expense or bill these events.

I expect legal clarity in transactions.

Primary Failure: Pricing Inconsistency
If the quoted price doesn’t match the final invoice, it’s a problem. I need consistency and documentation.

Structural Issue: Lack of Audit Trail
I need to see how pricing was derived and be able to reference it later. Without that, I can’t defend the expense.

Structural Issue: Hidden Terms
If fees or conditions aren’t visible upfront, it creates legal ambiguity.

Structural Issue: No Formal Documentation
Casual pricing without structured output (invoice, breakdown) is not acceptable.

Psychological Model
I optimize for: clarity, consistency, and defensibility
I refuse to compromise on: documented pricing
I avoid: ambiguous or inconsistent numbers
I evaluate tools by: legal and financial clarity

Pass / Fail Conditions
For this system to work for me, it must:

Provide clear, consistent pricing from configuration to final invoice.

Maintain a full audit trail of pricing decisions.

Display all fees and terms upfront.

Generate formal, structured invoices automatically.

Ensure no discrepancy between quoted and charged amounts.

Preserve pricing records for future reference.

Support defensible financial documentation.
