Client Profile: "Hiro Tanaka" - Staff iOS Engineer, Wallet & Identity Systems (Native-First)

If your payment flow feels like a web form, you’ve already lost me. I expect native OS-level integrations - wallets, autofill, identity - everything handled by the device.

Business Reality
Right now:

I book 4–6 private dining experiences per year, usually 2–6 guests.

Budget ranges from $1,200 to $5,500.

I expect native interactions - Face ID, Apple Pay, saved identity - no friction.

Dietary needs are minimal but should sync automatically.

Locations vary but should not change UX quality.

I operate almost entirely on iPhone.

I expect platform-native behavior everywhere.

Primary Failure: Non-Native Checkout
The biggest issue is when payment feels like a generic web experience. I don’t want to type, scroll, or fill forms. The OS already solved identity and payments - use it. If you don’t, your system feels outdated.

Structural Issue: Ignoring Device Capabilities
Modern devices offer identity, payment, and autofill APIs. Not using them is a design failure. I shouldn’t have to input information the device already knows.

Structural Issue: Fragmented Identity
I enter my name, email, address repeatedly. That’s unnecessary. Identity should be pulled from the device or account automatically.

Structural Issue: Inconsistent Platform Behavior
If the experience differs across iOS, Android, and desktop in quality, I lose trust. It should feel native everywhere.

Psychological Model
I optimize for: native integration and zero input
I refuse to compromise on: device-level payment and identity
I avoid: generic, non-native flows
I evaluate tools by: how well they leverage platform capabilities

Pass / Fail Conditions
For this system to work for me, it must:

Use native wallet integrations as the default payment method.

Pull identity data (name, email, address) from the device or account automatically.

Support biometric authentication for all payment actions.

Eliminate manual form entry wherever possible.

Maintain consistent native-quality UX across platforms.

Avoid fallback to generic web forms unless absolutely necessary.

Complete the booking flow with minimal taps using device capabilities.
