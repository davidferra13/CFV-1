Client Profile: "Julian Marks" - Growth Hacker, SaaS Startups (Velocity)

If I can’t move fast, I lose interest. Pricing should accelerate me, not slow me down.

Business Reality
Right now:

I book 4–8 private dinners per year, usually 2–10 guests.

Budget ranges from $1,000 to $6,000.

I expect to configure and pay quickly.

Dietary needs are minimal but must be captured.

Locations vary but should not slow the flow.

I act on impulse - speed matters.

I abandon anything that slows me down.

Primary Failure: Slow Decision Loop
The biggest issue is when pricing slows my decision-making. If I have to wait, interpret, or ask questions, I lose momentum. I want to move from idea to booking immediately.

Structural Issue: Too Many Steps
Multiple steps between configuration and pricing create friction. I want everything visible at once.

Structural Issue: Delayed Feedback
Any delay in pricing updates interrupts flow. I want continuous feedback.

Structural Issue: No Instant Action Path
Even when I see pricing, there’s often no immediate way to act. That’s wasted momentum.

Psychological Model
I optimize for: speed and momentum
I refuse to compromise on: immediate pricing and action
I avoid: slow or multi-step flows
I evaluate tools by: how fast I can complete the process

Pass / Fail Conditions
For this system to work for me, it must:

Display pricing immediately with no gating steps.

Update pricing continuously as I interact.

Minimize steps between configuration and payment.

Provide a clear, immediate action path to book.

Maintain fast, responsive interactions.

Avoid any delays or interruptions in flow.

Enable completion in a single, rapid session.
