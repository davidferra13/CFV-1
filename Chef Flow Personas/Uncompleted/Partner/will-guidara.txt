**Partner Profile: "Will Guidara" - Venue Collaboration Partner (Brand Curator)**

I am responsible for translating high-concept culinary experiences into profitable, repeatable, and visually stunning events. My role is to be the bridge between the raw talent, the physical location, the guest experience, and the financial outcome. I don't just host dinners; I build brand narratives that justify premium pricing and drive massive media buzz.

### Business Reality

Right now:
* I co-host, promote, or support **8 to 12** large-scale, high-profile events, referrals, or partnerships per **quarter**.
* We typically operate on a **40-60% revenue share** model, plus a mandatory minimum booking fee of **$25,000** per event, paid upfront.
* We frequently collaborate with major luxury brands (e.g., high-end spirits, fashion houses), unique venues (e.g., art galleries, private mansions), or celebrity talent for joint offers.
* Our event locations are highly varied, ranging from private rented estates to commercial gallery spaces, and availability constraints are dictated by the venue's existing calendar and the talent's schedule.
* Our current coordination setup is a chaotic mix of dedicated WhatsApp group chats (for day-of communication), Google Sheets (for guest lists and basic costing), and signed physical contracts (for legal agreements). There is absolutely no single source of truth.

---

### Primary Failure: Event-to-Profit Visibility Gap

The biggest operational problem is that I cannot accurately, in real-time, project the true profitability or necessary adjustments for an event once the initial booking is made. Because the revenue model is so complex—involving multiple revenue streams (ticket sales, sponsor contributions, alcohol minimums, and brand payments)—I have to manually reconcile everything using spreadsheets after the fact. This process is prone to error, takes my operations team 3-5 full days per event, and critically, it means that if a sponsor pulls funding or a variable cost (like staffing or unexpected vendor fees) changes, I don't know the immediate impact on the overall profit margin until the reconciliation is done. This severely limits my ability to confidently book the next, equally ambitious event.

---

### Structural Issue: Multi-Party Guest Experience Handoff

When we bring in a third-party venue (like a gallery) or a brand partner (like a liquor label), the guest experience flow is fragmented. The ticketing system, the venue’s check-in process, the brand's required pre-event communication, and the actual restaurant check-in process are all handled by separate systems. We currently use a combination of Eventbrite and a physical welcome packet, meaning my team doesn't have a unified view of who is attending, what their dietary restrictions are, which sponsors they are associated with, or if they have already signed a contract with the brand partner. This causes friction at the door, requires multiple staff members to check multiple lists, and degrades the luxury feel we are paid to provide.

---

### Structural Issue: Dynamic Menu Costing and Inventory Reconciliation

Because my events involve unique, temporary menus and often require sourcing hyper-local or specialized ingredients that change based on the venue or season, I cannot reliably calculate the final cost of goods sold (COGS) until the event is over. I currently build a preliminary menu cost in a separate spreadsheet, but I have no way to link that to the specific vendor invoices or the actual ingredient usage report from the kitchen *during* the event. This means that when I review the financials, the labor and food costs are disconnected from the specific, unique menu that was served, making accurate inventory management and vendor payment reconciliation nearly impossible.

---

### Structural Issue: Contract and Compliance Visibility

Every collaboration requires a unique set of contracts, liability waivers, and compliance checks (alcohol service permits, local health department approvals, etc.). These documents are generated by different legal teams (mine, the venue's, the brand's) and stored across various cloud drives or physical filing cabinets. When a new event is booked, I have to manually track which signed documents are needed, who is responsible for securing the permit, and when the deadline is. This process is slow, highly manual, and leads to last-minute panic calls because a necessary permit or signed rider was missed or filed incorrectly.

---

### Structural Issue: Staffing and Vendor Scheduling Overlap

My events are high-intensity, short-burst operations. Staffing needs change drastically between a "dinner service" and a "daytime tasting/rehearsal." I manage multiple groups of specialized talent (sommeliers, chefs, bartenders, brand liaisons) who are all booked for different shifts at different locations sometimes within the same week. I currently rely on a combination of shared email chains and physical whiteboard schedules, which means if one person calls out sick, or if a vendor needs to adjust their setup time, I have to manually call and reschedule 5-10 people, creating massive scheduling bottlenecks and potential service gaps.

---

### Psychological Model

I optimize for: **Brand integrity and perceived exclusivity.** The operation must feel effortless, expensive, and perfectly curated, regardless of the complexity behind the scenes.
I refuse to compromise on: **The guest journey.** If the guest feels friction, confusion, or inefficiency, the entire revenue stream suffers, no matter how good the food is.
I avoid: **Clunky, generic, or non-aesthetic technology.** If a tool looks like it belongs in an HR department, I will not trust it with my operations.
I evaluate tools by: **How quickly they allow me to scale the complexity without increasing the manual operational load.**

I think in terms of interconnected revenue streams and brand touchpoints. Every process—from the initial booking to the final bill—must feel like a seamless extension of the brand experience. If the tool adds friction or requires me to switch contexts (from the physical space to a digital system), it is a failure.

---

### Pass / Fail Conditions

For this system to work for me, it must:

1.  The system must **consolidate** all co-hosted event revenue streams (tickets, sponsors, minimums, merchandise sales) into a single, real-time financial ledger linked to the specific event instance.
2.  The system must **manage** a unified guest profile that combines ticketing data, contractual sponsor association, and required dietary/allergy notes for every attendee.
3.  The system must **host and track** all necessary vendor and venue compliance documents (permits, insurance riders, contracts) and trigger automated alerts 30 days, 7 days, and 24 hours before the deadline.
4.  The system must **allow me to define and execute** highly variable, multi-stage staffing schedules that account for different skill sets (e.g., "sommelier level 2," "gallery check-in staff") and automatically flag scheduling overlaps or gaps.
5.  The system must **integrate** the menu design and ingredient sourcing requirements directly into the cost calculation, allowing me to see a preliminary COGS estimate and adjust pricing *before* the event is even booked.
6.  The system must **support** multiple, distinct physical locations (venues) and allow me to map the operational flow (check-in, seating, service stations) for that specific, temporary event layout.
7.  The system must **provide** a centralized communication log tied to the specific event and partner, replacing the need for chaotic external messaging apps like WhatsApp.
