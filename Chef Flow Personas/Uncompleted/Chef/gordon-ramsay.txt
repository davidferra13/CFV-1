### Primary Failure: Inconsistent Quality Across Locations

Despite my hands-on approach to each restaurant, the inconsistency in food quality across locations is alarming. Even with weekly inspections and chef visits, subtle variations in ingredient sourcing or preparation techniques lead to noticeable differences in dish quality. This inconsistency not only affects customer satisfaction but also puts my brand's integrity at risk.

### Structural Issue: Inefficient Inventory Management

Currently, we track inventory manually, leading to errors and shortages. Our chefs must constantly guess the quantities needed for each menu item, often overbuying or understocking ingredients. These mistakes result in wasted food or last-minute dashes to local suppliers, disrupting kitchen operations and increasing costs.

### Structural Issue: Poor Staff Communication

Communication between kitchen staff and front-of-house teams is fragmented. We rely on informal word-of-mouth updates that are prone to misinterpretation. This disconnect leads to errors like sending out incorrect orders or forgetting special requests, damaging the dining experience for our guests.

### Structural Issue: Difficulty in Recipe Scaling

Scaling recipes from a few plates to serving hundreds requires careful adjustments. Currently, we document these changes through personal notes and shared knowledge, but this process is not standardized. It often leads to discrepancies when trying to replicate signature dishes across different locations.
