### Primary Failure: Event Logistics Overwhelm

The one biggest operational problem I face is the overwhelming nature of event logistics. Coordinating everything from sourcing ingredients to scheduling staff across multiple events in a week's time is a monumental task that often leads to last-minute scrambles, especially during peak season. The cost of not being able to plan efficiently can be seen in the form of overbudgeted events and dissatisfied clients.

### Structural Issue: Inefficient Inventory Management

My current workaround for inventory management involves a mix of handwritten lists and spreadsheets. While this method has served me well in the past, it becomes increasingly inefficient as I take on more clients and larger events. This system often leads to stockouts or overstocking, which can be costly and logistically challenging.

### Structural Issue: Limited Staff Scheduling Flexibility

Scheduling my team around multiple events while also accommodating their personal lives has been a significant challenge. My current method involves a lot of manual adjustments and last-minute changes, leading to staff dissatisfaction and the occasional miscommunication regarding event responsibilities.

### Structural Issue: Lack of Centralized Recipe Management

My recipe collection is scattered across various notebooks, files, and memory banks. This lack of centralized recipe management often leads to inconsistencies in menu execution and makes it difficult for my team to learn and adapt quickly to new dishes or variations.

### Structural Issue: Difficulty in Managing Client Expectations

With the increasing demand for personalized menus and unique dining experiences, managing client expectations has become more challenging than ever. My current method of communication relies heavily on emails and phone calls, which can be time-consuming and sometimes lead to misinterpretations or unmet expectations.
