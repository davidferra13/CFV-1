## PRIORITY 1: Menu Builder System (THE CORE)

This is the single most important feature in ChefFlow. No tool like this exists anywhere. Build it right.

### What It Must Do

A chef needs to build menus by pulling from their recipe library, see real-time food cost as they build, and have the system automatically generate shopping lists, prep lists, and cost breakdowns.

### The Chain (all connected, all live-updating)

```
Menu → Courses → Dishes → Recipes → Components → Ingredients (with portions, scaled by guest count)
```

Every level is connected. Change the guest count, everything recalculates. Swap a dish, the cost updates. Add a recipe, the shopping list grows.

### Tasks

- [ ] **Recipe Library** - Chef's personal recipe database with real portion sizes, yields, ingredients with costs, prep time, cook time, equipment needed. Recipes are entered manually by the chef (NEVER generated by AI). Each recipe has components (sub-recipes like sauces, stocks) and ingredients with exact quantities per portion.

- [ ] **Ingredient Database** - Master list of ingredients with current prices (manually entered by chef, updated over time). Unit conversions (oz to lb, ml to cups, etc.). Seasonal availability flags. Supplier/vendor tracking.

- [ ] **Menu Builder UI** - Drag-and-drop or click-to-add interface. Chef selects from their recipe library to build a menu. Organized by course (appetizer, main, dessert, etc.). Shows running food cost percentage as dishes are added. Guest count input that scales everything automatically.

- [ ] **Menu Costing Engine** - Real-time food cost calculation per dish, per course, per menu. Ingredient cost rolled up from recipe portions scaled to guest count. Waste factor calculation. Target food cost % with visual indicator (green/yellow/red). Ingredient overlap detection (efficiency: same ingredient used across courses).

- [ ] **Shopping List Generator** - Auto-generated from finalized menu + guest count. Consolidated across all recipes (no duplicate ingredients). Quantities scaled to portions. Organized by store section or vendor. Exportable/printable.

- [ ] **Prep List Generator** - Auto-generated from menu recipes. Timeline working backwards from service time. What can be done days ahead vs. day-of vs. last-minute. Task dependencies (stock must be made before sauce).

- [ ] **Menu Templates** - Save menus as templates for reuse. Clone and modify for similar events. Track which menus were used for which clients (never repeat for returning clients unless requested).

## PRIORITY 2: Inquiry Pipeline (Raw Feed to Refined Dashboard)

### What It Must Do

Every inquiry from every source funnels into one place. The chef sees a raw real-time feed, plus a refined dashboard for managing active leads.

### Tasks

- [ ] **Raw Inquiry Feed** - Real-time feed of every incoming inquiry, unfiltered. Shows source (website, email, Instagram, text, referral, marketplace). Timestamp, basic info preview. Think of it like a live activity monitor. Chef can see the system is working and nothing's being missed.

- [ ] **Refined Inquiry Dashboard** - Scored and organized view. Lead score (already built via GOLDMINE), follow-up status, response status. Filter by source, date, score, status. Quick-action buttons (respond, archive, convert to event).

- [ ] **Client History on Inquiry** - When an inquiry comes from a returning client, instantly surface: past events, menus served, dietary notes, preferences, last interaction date. No more scrolling through text messages to remember what they ate last year.

- [ ] **Multi-Source Intake** - Website embed (already built). Email (GOLDMINE already extracts). Add: manual entry form for phone/text inquiries. Future: Instagram DM integration, marketplace integrations.

## PRIORITY 3: Time Tracking

### What It Must Do

Track every hour the chef works, automatically categorized. Prep, shopping, travel, service, cleanup, admin, consultation. Show the chef their real hourly rate per event.

### Tasks

- [ ] **Time Entry** - Simple start/stop timer or manual entry. Categories: prep, shopping, travel, service, cleanup, admin, consultation, menu development, tasting. Attach to a specific event or mark as general business.

- [ ] **Per-Event Time Summary** - Total hours by category for each event. Calculated hourly rate (revenue minus food cost, divided by hours). Compare quoted time vs. actual time.

- [ ] **Business-Wide Time Dashboard** - Weekly/monthly hours by category. Average hourly rate across all events. Trends over time. Where time is being spent vs. where revenue comes from.

## PRIORITY 4: Quote Builder (Connected to Menu Costing)

### What It Must Do

Generate accurate quotes by pulling real costs from the menu builder, adding labor, travel, rentals, markup, and tax. No more guessing.

### Tasks

- [ ] **Quote Composition** - Auto-pull food cost from menu costing engine. Add labor (from time estimates or historical data). Add travel (mileage calculator or flat rate). Add rentals/equipment. Apply markup percentage. Calculate tax. Show total and per-head price.

- [ ] **Quote vs. Actual Reconciliation** - After event: compare quoted costs to actual spend. Where did the chef go over? Where was there margin? This feedback loop makes future quotes more accurate.

- [ ] **Quote Templates** - Save common quote structures. Seasonal pricing adjustments. Guest count tiers (pricing changes at 12, 25, 50 guests, etc.).

## PRIORITY 5: Communication Automation

### What It Must Do

Keep clients informed without the chef doing manual follow-ups. Automated but chef-approved communication at every stage.

### Tasks

- [ ] **Communication Timeline** - Visual timeline of all client touchpoints. Shows when the last contact was. Flags clients going dark (no response in X days). Flags when the chef is leaving a client in the dark.

- [ ] **Automated Follow-Ups** - Draft follow-up messages at key milestones (post-inquiry, post-tasting, 1 week before event, day after event). Chef reviews and sends (never auto-send without approval). Templates that feel personal, not robotic.

- [ ] **Client Portal Updates** - Clients can see their event status, menu (when approved), timeline. Reduces "hey just checking in" messages by 80%.

---

## PRIORITY 6: Financial Dashboard

### What It Must Do

Show the chef their complete financial picture. Revenue, expenses, profit, tax obligations, trends. Replace the spreadsheet and the accountant for day-to-day tracking.

### Tasks

- [ ] **Revenue Tracking** - Per-event revenue, monthly totals, year-over-year. By source (direct inquiry, referral, marketplace).
- [ ] **Expense Tracking** - Categorized expenses (food, labor, travel, equipment, insurance, marketing). Receipt capture. Tax-deductible flagging.
- [ ] **Profit Analysis** - Per-event profit margin. Monthly/quarterly P&L. Food cost % trends. Labor cost % trends.
- [ ] **Tax Prep** - Quarterly estimated tax calculations. Categorized deductions. Exportable reports for accountant.
