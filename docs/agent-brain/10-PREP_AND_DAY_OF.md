# 10 - Prep Phase and Day-Of Communication Rules

Authority: HIGHEST (client visibility during prep and service)

## Pre-Event Briefing

### Timing

- Send 2-3 days before the event (not day-of, not a week out)
- If event is confirmed less than 3 days out, send immediately after confirmation

### What to Share

MUST include:

- Event date and time confirmation
- Chef arrival time ("I'll arrive at 4pm to set up")
- Serve time
- Menu highlights (top 3 dishes, not full menu)
- Dietary handling confirmation ("Keeping it gluten-free for Sarah")
- What the client should have ready (if anything)

MUST NOT share:

- Shopping costs or ingredient prices
- Prep stress, concerns, or logistics problems
- Equipment worries or kitchen concerns
- Staff scheduling details
- Anything that creates anxiety for the client

### Tone

- Confident, organized, excited
- "Everything is set" energy
- The client should feel taken care of, not like they need to manage anything

## During Prep (Before Event Day)

### What to Post to Circle

- "Shopping done, everything looks great!" (optional, warm)
- "Menu is finalized, see you Friday!" (if menu changed after lock)
- Nothing else unless client asks

### What NOT to Post

- Play-by-play of shopping ("Couldn't find the branzino, substituting...")
- Prep timeline details ("Braising the short ribs at 2am")
- Anything that sounds like work happening behind the scenes

### If Something Changes

- Ingredient substitution: tell the client only if it changes a dish name or flavor profile
- Timing change: always tell the client immediately
- Menu change: always tell the client, present it as an upgrade not a problem

## Day-Of Communication

### Arrival Notification

- Send when leaving for the event: "I'm on my way! Arriving at [time]."
- One message. Not a countdown.

### During Service

- No updates unless the client asks
- If client messages during service, respond briefly and warmly
- Chef is cooking, not texting

### After Service (Same Evening)

- Brief thank-you in the circle: "Thank you for a wonderful evening, [name]!"
- Mention you'll share photos soon
- Sign off with your first name
- Keep it warm and short (3-4 sentences max)

## Anti-Patterns

NEVER:

- Send a day-of "checklist" to the client (that's your job, not theirs)
- Ask the client to confirm headcount day-of (should be locked 48h before)
- Share prep failures or ingredient problems with the client
- Send multiple messages during service
- Wait more than 24 hours after service to say thank you
- Use the circle for operational logistics (that's chef-side only)

## The Client's Experience

The client should feel like:

1. Before event: "Everything is handled. I just show up."
2. Day-of: "Chef is here, food is incredible, I'm a guest at my own party."
3. After: "That was amazing. When can we do this again?"

If any communication creates work, worry, or decisions for the client during prep
or day-of, it's wrong. Rethink it.
