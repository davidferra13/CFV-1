# 11 - Post-Event Communication Rules

Authority: HIGHEST (relationship nurturing after service)

## Post-Event Timeline

| When         | What                       | Channel       |
| ------------ | -------------------------- | ------------- |
| Same evening | Thank-you in circle        | Dinner Circle |
| Within 24h   | Share photos               | Dinner Circle |
| 3 days       | Thank-you email            | Email         |
| 7 days       | Review/testimonial request | Email         |
| 14 days      | Referral ask               | Email         |
| Seasonal     | Re-engagement              | Email         |

## Same-Evening Thank-You (Circle)

### Rules

- Post to circle within hours of service ending
- Personal, warm, specific to the evening
- Mention the client by first name
- Say you'll share photos soon
- Sign off with chef's first name
- 3-4 sentences max

### Template Structure

1. "Thank you for a wonderful evening, {clientFirst}!"
2. Optional: one specific moment or dish highlight
3. "I'll share photos here soon."
4. "{chefFirst}"

### What NOT to Do

- Don't ask for a review in the same-evening message
- Don't mention money, invoices, or payments
- Don't ask about the food quality ("How was everything?")
- Don't be overly formal or use business language

## Photos (Circle, Within 24h)

- Share 5-10 best photos, not every photo taken
- Brief caption: "Here are some highlights from the evening!"
- If photos aren't ready within 24h, that's fine, but don't go beyond 48h

## Thank-You Email (3 Days)

- Warm, personal, not transactional
- Reference the occasion and a specific highlight
- Do NOT include: review links, referral asks, upsells, booking prompts
- This email has ONE job: make the client feel valued

## Review/Testimonial Request (7 Days)

### Tone

- Grateful, not transactional
- "If you have a moment" energy, not "please leave us a 5-star review"
- Frame it as helping future clients find the right chef
- One link. One ask. No guilt.

### Good

"If you have a moment, a short note about your experience helps future clients
find the right chef for their events. Here's a quick link: [review URL]"

### Bad

"Please leave a 5-star review! Reviews help our business grow!"
"Don't forget to leave a review!"

## Referral Ask (14 Days)

### Tone

- Organic, not salesy
- "If you know anyone..." not "Refer 3 friends for 10% off"
- No formal referral program language unless chef has one set up
- Keep it in the same warm tone as all other communication

### Good

"If any friends or family are looking for a private chef for an upcoming event,
I'd love to help. Just point them my way."

### Bad

"Refer a friend and get $50 off your next booking!"
"Know someone who needs a chef? Use code REFER50!"

## Re-Engagement (Seasonal/Occasion-Based)

### When

- Based on occasions (birthday coming up? Anniversary season?)
- Based on seasons (summer grilling? Holiday entertaining?)
- NOT on a fixed calendar ("It's been 90 days since your last event")

### Tone

- "Thought of you because..." not "We haven't heard from you in a while"
- Specific to something the chef knows about the client
- No urgency, no FOMO, no limited-time offers

## What NEVER Goes to the Client Post-Event

- AAR (After Action Review) content (that's chef-internal)
- Financial breakdowns or food cost analysis
- Staff performance notes
- Prep timeline retrospective
- Anything the chef would discuss with their business partner, not their client

## Dinner Circle Post-Event Usage

The circle stays active after the event for:

- Photos
- Thank-you message
- Client replies and feedback
- Future event planning ("Want to do this again for the holidays?")

The circle does NOT get:

- Review request (keep that in email, less pressure)
- Referral ask (email only)
- Invoice or payment reminders (portal handles those)
