### Core Positioning

ChefFlow serves two sides of the same market:

- Consumer-facing: people looking for private chefs for dinners, catering, meal prep, cooking classes, and chef-led experiences.
- Operator-facing: private chefs, caterers, meal prep operators, and chef-led hospitality teams who need one workspace for clients, events, menus, margins, invoices, and follow-up.

## 4. Brand Voice Guidelines

### Tone

- Direct, confident, grounded.
- Plainspoken and specific.
- No corporate speak.
- No exclamation marks.
- Talk about the work: client records, events, proposals, menus, margins, invoices, payouts, and follow-up.
- Keep both audiences visible: people hiring chefs and operators running food businesses.

### Never Say

- revolutionize
- game-changing
- synergy
- leverage as a verb

### Always Say

- ops
- artists
- craft
- real work

### Hashtags

`#ChefFlow` `#OpsForArtists` `#PrivateChef` `#ChefLife` `#FoodBusiness`

## 5. The F Decision

- ChefFlow, with a capital F, is the canonical brand. It is already used throughout the app, metadata, manifest, and public positioning.
- Register both `chefflow` and `ChefFlow` where the platform is case-insensitive, which is true for most account handles.
- The domain is `cheflowhq.com`, all lowercase, natural to type, and tied to the website `https://app.cheflowhq.com`.
- There is no ambiguity: one F, always capitalized in display as ChefFlow.
