## Detailed Scoring

### 🟢 Strengths (What works well for Sean)

- **Inventory Tracking:** The detailed ingredient tracking and waste logging are excellent for cost control, which is crucial for high-margin pop-ups.

### 🟡 Weaknesses (What needs improvement for Sean)

- **Real-Time Adaptability:** The system feels too linear. When a key ingredient fails to arrive, he needs a "swap-out" function that instantly suggests 3 viable, complementary menu adjustments, rather than just flagging an error.
- **Experiential Storytelling:** The platform is transactional. It lacks a space to build the _narrative_ around the menu—the sourcing story, the inspiration, the chef's notes—which is part of the premium experience he sells.
- **Cross-Platform Communication:** He needs seamless integration with high-end booking platforms (like Tock or Resy) that allow for dynamic menu updates visible to the customer _before_ they book.

### 🔴 Critical Gaps (Dealbreakers)

- **Lack of "Flex Mode":** The system needs a "Flex Mode" or "Emergency Override" that allows him to manually override cost calculations or inventory counts with a simple, auditable reason (e.g., "Chef's discretion: Use 10% more truffle oil for presentation").
