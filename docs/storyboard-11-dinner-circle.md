# Storyboard #11: The Dinner Circle - Plan a Dinner with Friends

> "Your clients don't book alone. Their friends do too."
> Duration: 90 seconds | Format: 1920x1080 (YouTube 16:9) | Auth: client + guest links

---

## The pitch in one sentence

One client creates a dinner group, invites 4 friends, friends join via link, the group coordinates dates and dietary needs in a shared space, and the chef gets a fully organized booking with a complete guest list and dietary matrix. One happy client becomes five future clients.

## A-HA MOMENTS (4 total)

1. **Scale:** One booking from a dinner circle brings 5 guests, each of whom becomes a contact with dietary preferences saved.
2. **Intelligence:** The dietary matrix is auto-populated from guest RSVPs. The chef sees every allergy without asking.
3. **Both sides:** Guests join without downloading an app or creating an account. Friction-free.
4. **Money:** Lead score boosted for dinner circle bookings. Groups mean serious, high-value events.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- The Hub/Dinner Circle feature must be functional enough to show: group creation, invite flow, availability grid, group chat, and booking submission. If parts aren't built, simulate with pre-seeded data.
- The "friend joins" montage (Scene 4, 0:34-0:36) can be done with quick cuts showing pre-filled forms. Each friend's form is a separate recording stitched together, or use page reloads with different pre-filled data.
- The availability grid widget is visually important. If it's not built, a simple calendar with colored dots (green = available) works.
- Group chat messages should be pre-seeded for the recording. Real-time chat between recording contexts is too complex to coordinate.
- The referral tracking / viral loop scene (Scene 7) is the emotional payoff. If referral tags aren't in the client list, this can be shown as inquiry source attribution instead.
- This is a longer video (90s) with the most perspective switches. Tight pacing is essential. The montage in Scene 4 keeps it from dragging.
