# Storyboard #12: Share Your Chef

> "Your best marketing is your clients talking about you."
> Duration: 60 seconds | Format: 1920x1080 (YouTube 16:9) | Auth: client + new prospect (public)

---

## The pitch in one sentence

A happy client shares their chef with a friend using a referral link. The friend lands on the chef's profile, submits an inquiry, and the chef sees a new lead tagged "referred by Sarah" with a boosted lead score. Word-of-mouth becomes trackable and automatic.

## A-HA MOMENTS (3 total)

1. **Scale:** One referral creates a full pipeline entry with attribution. The chef sees exactly who referred whom.
2. **Intelligence:** Lead score boosted for referrals. The system knows referred leads convert better.
3. **Delight:** The referring client earns loyalty points. Referrals are incentivized and tracked automatically.

## CAPTION COUNT: 10 total

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- Three contexts: client (Sarah), friend/prospect (public, no auth), chef. Record the prospect's view on the public chef profile page (no login needed).
- The referral tracking link must carry attribution. If the URL includes a referral parameter (e.g., `?ref=sarah123`), the inquiry created from it should show the referral source.
- If referral loyalty point rewards aren't built, show them as a simulated notification on Sarah's client portal.
- The chef profile page is public and should look polished: real photo, bio, specialties. Seed this for the agent/demo chef account.
- The "via Sarah M." tag on the inquiry is the key visual. If referral attribution isn't built into the inquiry pipeline view, show it on the inquiry detail page as a manual tag or note.
