# ChefFlow Market Sizing: TAM / SAM / SOM

**Date:** March 15, 2026
**Purpose:** Comprehensive market sizing for ChefFlow as a universal food services platform
**Scope:** Global TAM, U.S. SAM, realistic SOM for Years 1-3

---

## Executive Summary

ChefFlow sits at the intersection of three broken markets: (1) hiring chefs is hard, (2) finding food options is fragmented, and (3) no single platform connects all food services. The platform's addressable market spans from private chef ops tools (current product) to the entire food services technology ecosystem.

| Level   | Market                                          | Size                                            | Notes                                           |
| ------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **TAM** | Global food services + food tech                | **$4.0T+ food services; $6.5B restaurant SaaS** | Everything ChefFlow could theoretically address |
| **SAM** | U.S. food services tech + hiring                | **$12.8B - $18.5B**                             | Segments ChefFlow actually serves               |
| **SOM** | Private chef ops + adjacent archetypes (Yr 1-3) | **$8M - $45M**                                  | Realistic capture with current product          |

## 3. SOM: Serviceable Obtainable Market (Years 1-3)

The SOM represents what ChefFlow can realistically capture in the near term, given its current product (private chef ops tools) and planned expansion path.

### 3a. Year 1: Private Chef Ops Tools (Current Product)

**Target:** Private and personal chefs who need business management software.

| Assumption                           | Value                         | Basis                                          |
| ------------------------------------ | ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Total private/personal chefs in U.S. | ~20,000 (midpoint estimate)   | Zippia, APPCA, industry associations           |
| Chefs actively seeking ops tools     | ~30% (6,000)                  | Many still use spreadsheets/paper              |
| Realistic Year 1 conversion          | 1% - 3% (60 - 180 chefs)      | Early-stage SaaS with 4 beta testers currently |
| Average annual revenue per chef      | $600 - $1,200/yr ($50-100/mo) | SaaS subscription pricing                      |
| **Year 1 SOM**                       | **$36K - $216K**              | Conservative; focused on product-market fit    |

### 3b. Year 2: Expanding to Adjacent Chef Archetypes

**Target:** Caterers, event chefs, food truck operators who share similar ops needs (quoting, invoicing, client management, event tracking).

| Assumption                                                     | Value                                                  | Basis                                    |
| -------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------- |
| Addressable providers (private chefs + caterers + event chefs) | ~53,000 (20K chefs + 13K caterers + 20K event-focused) | Combined industry data                   |
| Chefs/caterers seeking ops tools                               | ~35% (18,500)                                          | Slightly higher, market more mature      |
| Realistic Year 2 conversion                                    | 2% - 5% (370 - 925 providers)                          | Growing brand, referrals, proven product |
| Average annual revenue per provider                            | $900 - $1,500/yr                                       | Higher ARPU as feature set expands       |
| Marketplace take rate on bookings                              | 5% - 10%                                               | If marketplace features launch           |
| **Year 2 SOM**                                                 | **$330K - $1.4M** (SaaS only)                          | Before marketplace GMV                   |

### 3c. Year 3: Platform + Marketplace

**Target:** All independent food service providers (chefs, caterers, food trucks) plus consumer-facing discovery/booking.

| Assumption                                                   | Value                                      | Basis                                          |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Total addressable providers (chefs + caterers + food trucks) | ~85,000 - 125,000                          | Combined data across segments                  |
| Platform adoption                                            | 3% - 7% (2,550 - 8,750 providers)          | Network effects kicking in                     |
| SaaS ARPU                                                    | $1,200 - $2,000/yr                         | Full-featured platform                         |
| Marketplace GMV per active provider                          | $50,000 - $150,000/yr                      | Average event revenue flowing through platform |
| Take rate on marketplace GMV                                 | 5% - 8%                                    | Industry standard for services marketplaces    |
| **Year 3 SOM (SaaS)**                                        | **$3M - $17.5M**                           | Subscription revenue                           |
| **Year 3 SOM (Marketplace)**                                 | **$6.4M - $52.5M GMV; $320K - $4.2M take** | Booking/transaction revenue                    |
| **Year 3 Combined SOM**                                      | **$3.3M - $21.7M revenue**                 | SaaS + marketplace take                        |

### 4d. ChefFlow's Differentiation

No existing platform does all three:

1. **Ops tools for the chef** (business management, not just a listing)
2. **Marketplace for the consumer** (find and book any food service)
3. **Universal food discovery** (restaurants, chefs, caterers, food trucks in one place)

Toast is closest on ops but only serves restaurants. CookUnity is closest on chef marketplace but only does meal delivery. Yelp is closest on discovery but doesn't provide ops tools or handle bookings end-to-end. ChefFlow's thesis is that the winner will be the platform that unifies all three.

## 5. Key Takeaways

### The Funnel

```
TAM:  $4.0T global food services + $6.5B restaurant SaaS
       |
SAM:  $12.8B - $18.5B (U.S. food tech/platform layer)
       |
SOM:  Yr 1: $36K - $216K (private chef SaaS)
      Yr 2: $330K - $1.4M (+ caterers, event chefs)
      Yr 3: $3.3M - $21.7M (platform + marketplace)
```

| Market                 | CAGR          | Period    |
| ---------------------- | ------------- | --------- |
| Restaurant SaaS        | 14.7% - 15.0% | 2025-2030 |
| Personal chef services | 6.7% - 9.5%   | 2025-2033 |
| Online food delivery   | 11.9%         | 2025-2030 |
| U.S. catering          | 5.2% - 7.7%   | 2025-2030 |
| Meal kit delivery      | 7.7% - 8.8%   | 2025-2033 |

### Why This Matters

The private chef segment alone ($1.3B, growing to $6.2B by 2030) justifies ChefFlow's initial focus. But the real opportunity is the platform play: no one has unified ops tools, consumer discovery, and booking across all food service types. The total U.S. technology/platform opportunity across these segments is $12-18B and growing at 10%+ annually. If ChefFlow captures even 0.1% of the SAM, that is $12M-$18M in annual revenue.
