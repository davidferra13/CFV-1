# Service Lifecycle Blueprint

> **The complete, expanded architecture of every stage, checkpoint, and decision point in a private chef engagement, from first contact through long-term client relationship.**
>
> Not every checkpoint applies to every service. This is the full universe. Chefs configure which stages and checkpoints are active for their workflow.

---

## Stage 1: Inquiry Received

The moment a potential client makes contact. Speed matters: every hour without a response increases the chance of losing the booking.

- [ ] Date and time of inquiry captured
- [ ] Source tracked (website form, email, phone call, text, DM, referral, third-party platform)
- [ ] Referral source noted (if referred, by whom)
- [ ] Host name captured
- [ ] Host email captured
- [ ] Host phone number captured
- [ ] Preferred communication channel identified (email, text, phone, app)
- [ ] Event date captured (or flexible date range)
- [ ] Guest count captured (including host)
- [ ] Event location captured (full address or TBD)
- [ ] Event type identified (intimate dinner, party, holiday, corporate, date night, proposal, wedding, milestone birthday, etc.)
- [ ] How they heard about you noted
- [ ] Initial budget range mentioned (if volunteered)
- [ ] Any immediate requests or special notes captured
- [ ] Inquiry assigned to correct chef (if multi-chef operation)
- [ ] Auto-acknowledgment sent (if automated)
- [ ] Personal initial response sent (target: within 24 hours, ideal: within 2 hours)
- [ ] Response time tracked (for performance metrics)

---

## Stage 2: Discovery

The information-gathering phase. The goal is to collect everything needed to build an accurate quote and a menu the client will love. This stage can happen over one conversation or several.

### Guest and Dietary Information

- [ ] Full guest list collected (names, not just count)
- [ ] Per-guest dietary restrictions collected (allergies, intolerances, religious dietary laws, lifestyle choices)
- [ ] Severity of restrictions confirmed (allergy vs. preference vs. life-threatening)
- [ ] Children attending (ages, separate menu needed?)
- [ ] Dietary information confirmed directly with guests (not just host's memory)

### Cuisine and Menu Preferences

- [ ] Cuisine preferences gathered (likes, dislikes, cravings, themes)
- [ ] Cuisine dislikes or hard-no ingredients noted
- [ ] Favorite restaurants or dishes mentioned (style indicators)
- [ ] Previous private chef experiences discussed (what they liked/didn't)
- [ ] Comfort level with adventurous food assessed
- [ ] Course count discussed (appetizers, 3-course, 5-course, tasting menu, etc.)
- [ ] Service style preference confirmed (plated, family style, buffet, stations, passed apps, interactive)

### Budget and Expectations

- [ ] Budget discussed (per person, flat rate, or open-ended)
- [ ] What's included in the price explained (groceries, travel, service, cleanup)
- [ ] Grocery cost model explained (included in flat rate vs. pass-through with receipts)
- [ ] Gratuity policy communicated
- [ ] Client expectations for the experience discussed (what does success look like to them?)

### Drinks and Beverage

- [ ] Drinks/bar expectations discussed (BYOB, chef-selected pairings, non-alcoholic options, not involved)
- [ ] Wine/cocktail pairing interest level
- [ ] Beverage budget (if separate from food)
- [ ] Bar setup needs (glassware, ice, mixers)

### Venue and Kitchen

- [ ] Kitchen situation discussed (full kitchen, outdoor, limited equipment, commercial)
- [ ] Kitchen visit needed? (first-time venue flag)
- [ ] Dining space discussed (indoor, outdoor, table size, seating arrangement)
- [ ] Equipment the chef needs to bring vs. what's on-site
- [ ] Parking and loading access discussed
- [ ] Elevator or stairs (for equipment transport)

### Event Details

- [ ] Special occasion details captured (birthday, anniversary, proposal, holiday, corporate milestone)
- [ ] Surprise element? (if so, coordination details)
- [ ] Vibe/atmosphere preferences discussed (casual, formal, interactive, intimate, party)
- [ ] Dress code for chef/staff (if corporate or formal)
- [ ] Table setting/presentation expectations discussed (simple, elevated, themed)
- [ ] Flowers, decor, or other vendor coordination needed?
- [ ] Photography/social media preferences discussed (can chef photograph and post? restrictions?)
- [ ] Confidentiality requirements (NDA needed? high-profile client?)

### Communication Preferences

- [ ] Primary contact person confirmed (host, assistant, event planner)
- [ ] Best way to reach them confirmed
- [ ] Response time expectations set (both directions)
- [ ] Social media consent obtained (can you tag them, share photos of the dinner?)

### Tasting/Trial Dinner (if applicable)

- [ ] Tasting dinner offered (common for high-value events, weddings, retainer clients)
- [ ] Tasting date scheduled
- [ ] Tasting menu planned
- [ ] Tasting completed
- [ ] Tasting feedback received

---

## Stage 3: Quote

Translating discovery into a price. Clarity and professionalism here set the tone for the entire engagement.

- [ ] Quote drafted (itemized breakdown or flat rate, per your style)
- [ ] Quote includes: food cost, service fee, travel (if applicable), equipment rental (if applicable), staff (if applicable)
- [ ] Grocery cost model clear (included vs. pass-through)
- [ ] Gratuity policy included
- [ ] Cancellation/reschedule policy included or referenced
- [ ] Quote validity period stated (e.g., "valid for 7 days")
- [ ] Quote sent to host
- [ ] Quote follow-up sent (if no response within 48 hours)
- [ ] Quote reviewed by host
- [ ] Questions answered
- [ ] Revisions requested (if any)
- [ ] Revised quote sent (if applicable)
- [ ] Quote accepted and confirmed (verbal or written)
- [ ] Deposit amount and terms communicated
- [ ] Deposit invoice sent
- [ ] Deposit received
- [ ] Deposit confirmed and receipt sent

---

## Stage 4: Agreement

The formal commitment. Protects both the chef and the client. Complexity scales with the event (a casual dinner for 4 may need a simple confirmation; a 50-person corporate event needs a full contract).

- [ ] Service agreement/contract drafted
- [ ] Agreement includes: scope of service, date, time, location, guest count, pricing, payment schedule
- [ ] Cancellation policy detailed (tiered by timeframe)
- [ ] Reschedule policy detailed
- [ ] Liability and insurance terms included
- [ ] Kitchen access and condition terms included
- [ ] Grocery reimbursement model formalized
- [ ] Confidentiality/NDA clause included (if required)
- [ ] Social media and photography rights addressed
- [ ] Force majeure clause included (illness, weather, emergencies)
- [ ] Dispute resolution process included
- [ ] Intellectual property clause (chef retains recipe IP)
- [ ] Agreement sent to client
- [ ] Agreement reviewed by client
- [ ] Questions or revisions addressed
- [ ] Agreement signed by client
- [ ] Agreement countersigned by chef
- [ ] Signed copy stored and accessible to both parties
- [ ] Insurance certificate provided to venue (if required)

---

## Stage 5: Menu Planning

The creative stage. This is where the chef's artistry meets the client's preferences.

- [ ] Rough draft menu created (based on all discovery information)
- [ ] Menu accounts for every dietary restriction collected
- [ ] Rough draft sent to host for feedback
- [ ] Host feedback received
- [ ] Menu revised based on feedback (if needed)
- [ ] Second draft sent (if significant changes)
- [ ] Course timing and flow planned (apps arrival, gaps between courses, dessert timing)
- [ ] Plating style decided for each course
- [ ] Wine/cocktail pairings selected (if applicable)
- [ ] Pairing notes/descriptions written (if sharing with guests)
- [ ] Amuse-bouche or welcome bite planned (if applicable)
- [ ] Bread/butter service planned (if applicable)
- [ ] Intermezzo planned (if multi-course tasting)
- [ ] Dessert and after-dinner offerings planned (coffee, tea, petit fours, digestif)
- [ ] Menu descriptions written (client-facing, for printed menus or digital share)
- [ ] Final menu confirmed by host
- [ ] Final menu locked (no more changes after this point)
- [ ] Menu shared with guests (if host wants that, via dinner circle or direct share)
- [ ] Printed or digital menu cards prepared (if applicable)

---

## Stage 6: Pre-Service Logistics

Everything between confirmed menu and walking in the door. This is where operational excellence prevents day-of chaos.

### Final Confirmations

- [ ] Final guest count confirmed (48-72 hours before)
- [ ] Final dietary re-check (any new allergies, last-minute guest changes)
- [ ] Final menu adjustments (if guest count changed significantly)
- [ ] Arrival time confirmed with host
- [ ] Parking and access logistics confirmed
- [ ] Confirmation message sent to host (day before or morning of)
- [ ] Host reminded of anything they need to provide (table setup, specific equipment, BYOB)

### Shopping and Procurement

- [ ] Shopping list built from final menu
- [ ] Grocery budget approved (if pass-through model)
- [ ] Sourcing preferences confirmed (organic, local, kosher, halal, specific purveyors)
- [ ] Substitution protocol agreed upon (can chef substitute if item unavailable? call first?)
- [ ] Specialty items ordered in advance (if needed)
- [ ] Groceries purchased
- [ ] Grocery receipts saved (for pass-through billing)
- [ ] All items verified against shopping list
- [ ] Cold chain maintained (proper transport and storage)

### Equipment and Setup

- [ ] Equipment checklist prepared (what to bring)
- [ ] Equipment packed and loaded
- [ ] Rentals arranged (if applicable: plates, linens, glassware, chafing dishes)
- [ ] Rental delivery and pickup confirmed
- [ ] Disposables purchased (if applicable)
- [ ] Uniforms/chef coat prepared

### Staff Coordination (if applicable)

- [ ] Sous chef/assistant sourced and confirmed
- [ ] Servers sourced and confirmed
- [ ] Bartender sourced and confirmed (if applicable)
- [ ] Cleanup crew confirmed (if separate from cooking staff)
- [ ] Staff briefed on menu, timing, dietary restrictions, service style
- [ ] Staff arrival time confirmed
- [ ] Staff dress code communicated
- [ ] Staff payment terms confirmed
- [ ] Background checks completed (if required for high-profile clients)

### Prep Schedule

- [ ] Prep timeline built (what gets done when)
- [ ] Prep-ahead items identified (what can be done day before)
- [ ] Day-before prep completed
- [ ] Day-of prep timeline confirmed
- [ ] Course-by-course execution timeline built (first app at 7:00, second course at 7:45, etc.)
- [ ] Cleanup timeline estimated

### Vendor Coordination (if applicable)

- [ ] Event planner synced on timeline
- [ ] Florist delivery time confirmed
- [ ] Photographer arrival time confirmed
- [ ] Rental company delivery/pickup times confirmed
- [ ] Beverage vendor delivery confirmed
- [ ] Any other vendor timelines aligned

---

## Stage 7: Payment

Financial clarity at every step. No surprises for either party.

- [ ] Deposit already received (from Stage 3)
- [ ] Remaining balance calculated
- [ ] Grocery costs finalized (if pass-through, receipts compiled)
- [ ] Any additional charges itemized (extra guests, special requests, rentals)
- [ ] Any credits applied (overpayment, issue resolution)
- [ ] Final invoice sent
- [ ] Payment method confirmed
- [ ] Payment due date communicated (pre-service, day-of, or net terms for corporate)
- [ ] Final payment received
- [ ] Payment confirmed and receipt sent
- [ ] Grocery receipts shared with client (if pass-through model)
- [ ] Gratuity received (if applicable)
- [ ] Gratuity distributed to staff (if applicable)
- [ ] Tax documentation prepared (if required)
- [ ] Corporate invoicing handled (net-30, purchase orders, etc. if applicable)
- [ ] Financial reconciliation complete (actual costs vs. estimated, margin calculated)

---

## Stage 8: Service Day

Execution. Everything before this was preparation. This is the performance.

### Arrival and Setup

- [ ] Arrived at venue on time
- [ ] Venue walkthrough completed (first visit) or kitchen verified (return visit)
- [ ] Equipment unloaded and organized
- [ ] Kitchen workspace set up
- [ ] Staff arrived and briefed
- [ ] Ingredients organized and prepped for first course
- [ ] Table/dining area inspected (if chef is involved in presentation)

### Service Execution

- [ ] Welcome interaction with host (check in, confirm timeline, last-minute notes)
- [ ] Amuse-bouche/welcome bite served (if applicable)
- [ ] Each course executed on timeline
- [ ] Dietary-restricted plates correctly identified and served to right guests
- [ ] Pacing adjusted based on guest energy (conversation flowing? ready for next course?)
- [ ] Wine/cocktail pairings served with correct courses (if applicable)
- [ ] Real-time adjustments noted (substitutions, timing changes, guest requests)
- [ ] Chef interacted with guests (if appropriate for the vibe)
- [ ] Menu cards or course descriptions presented (if applicable)

### Cleanup and Departure

- [ ] Kitchen cleaned to pre-arrival condition (or better)
- [ ] All equipment packed
- [ ] Trash taken out (don't leave it for the host)
- [ ] Leftovers packaged and labeled (if applicable)
- [ ] Leftover policy communicated (what stays, what goes)
- [ ] Final check-in with host before leaving
- [ ] Departure time noted
- [ ] Rentals staged for pickup (if applicable)
- [ ] Nothing left behind (equipment, personal items)

---

## Stage 9: Post-Service

The experience doesn't end when you leave. This stage builds the relationship and generates future business.

### Immediate Follow-Up (within 24 hours)

- [ ] Thank you message sent to host
- [ ] Personal touch included (reference a specific moment from the dinner)
- [ ] Photos shared (if taken during service)
- [ ] Leftover storage/reheating instructions sent (if applicable)

### Review and Feedback (within 48-72 hours)

- [ ] Review request sent (Google, website, Yelp, or wherever you collect them)
- [ ] Specific review link provided (make it frictionless)
- [ ] Review received
- [ ] Review responded to publicly (if on a public platform)
- [ ] Testimonial permission requested (for website/marketing use)
- [ ] Negative feedback addressed (if any, directly and professionally)

### Financial Close-Out

- [ ] All payments received and reconciled
- [ ] Outstanding balance followed up on (if applicable)
- [ ] Grocery reconciliation shared (if pass-through model, actuals vs. estimate)
- [ ] Overage/underage resolved
- [ ] Tax documentation finalized

### Internal Debrief

- [ ] After-action notes captured (what went well, what to improve)
- [ ] Guest preferences noted for future bookings (who loved what, who didn't eat X)
- [ ] Venue notes updated (kitchen quirks, parking tips, equipment gaps)
- [ ] Recipe notes updated (any modifications made during service)
- [ ] Staff performance noted (who to rebook, who to skip)
- [ ] Time tracking completed (total hours: prep, travel, service, cleanup)
- [ ] Profitability calculated (revenue minus all costs including time)

---

## Stage 10: Client Lifecycle

The long game. This is where one-time clients become regulars, and regulars become advocates.

### Client Profile Maintenance

- [ ] Client profile updated with all preferences, restrictions, and notes
- [ ] Client tier classified (one-time, occasional, regular, VIP, corporate)
- [ ] Preferred menu styles documented (for faster future planning)
- [ ] Preferred communication style noted
- [ ] Key dates captured (birthdays, anniversaries, annual events)
- [ ] Household changes tracked (new baby, new dietary needs, moved addresses)

### Rebooking and Retention

- [ ] Rebooking follow-up sent (2-4 weeks after service)
- [ ] Seasonal outreach sent (holiday menus, summer grilling, etc.)
- [ ] Anniversary of first booking acknowledged (personal touch)
- [ ] Recurring booking template created (for regular clients)
- [ ] Loyalty program engagement tracked (if applicable)
- [ ] Win-back campaign triggered (if no booking in X months)

### Referral and Growth

- [ ] Referral request made (after positive experience)
- [ ] Referral tracked (who referred whom)
- [ ] Referral reward sent (if referral program exists)
- [ ] Client featured in marketing (with permission: testimonial, case study, social post)

## Cancellation and Reschedule Workflow

> **This is not a stage. It is a parallel workflow that can trigger from any stage.** Every stage has different financial and operational consequences for cancellation.

### Cancellation

- [ ] Cancellation request received
- [ ] Reason for cancellation noted
- [ ] Current stage identified (determines refund/penalty)
- [ ] Cancellation policy applied:
  - [ ] Before deposit: full cancel, no penalty
  - [ ] After deposit, before groceries purchased: deposit retained per policy
  - [ ] After groceries purchased: deposit + grocery costs retained
  - [ ] Within 48 hours of service: full payment may be due per policy
  - [ ] Day-of cancellation: full payment due
- [ ] Refund calculated and communicated
- [ ] Refund processed
- [ ] Cancellation confirmation sent
- [ ] Staff notified (if already booked)
- [ ] Vendor cancellations made (if applicable)
- [ ] Groceries handled (return, repurpose, donate)
- [ ] Date freed up on calendar
- [ ] Client retained in system for future outreach (unless they request removal)
- [ ] Cancellation reason tracked (for pattern analysis)

### Reschedule

- [ ] Reschedule request received
- [ ] New date proposed
- [ ] Chef availability confirmed for new date
- [ ] Reschedule policy applied (fee, no fee, deadline-dependent)
- [ ] New date confirmed
- [ ] All logistics updated (staff, vendors, rentals)
- [ ] Client confirmation sent with new details
- [ ] Original date freed on calendar

## Chef Profile (One-Time Setup, Ongoing Maintenance)

> **These are not per-event checkpoints. They are business-level items that enable everything above.**

- [ ] Business entity established (LLC, sole proprietor, etc.)
- [ ] Food handler/ServSafe certification current
- [ ] Business license current
- [ ] General liability insurance active ($1M+ recommended)
- [ ] Product liability insurance active
- [ ] Workers' compensation insurance (if hiring staff)
- [ ] Commercial auto insurance (if transporting food)
- [ ] Certificate of insurance template ready (venues often request this)
- [ ] Health department permits (jurisdiction-dependent)
- [ ] Standard contract template created
- [ ] Standard cancellation policy defined
- [ ] Standard pricing model established
- [ ] Standard gratuity policy defined
- [ ] Business bank account set up
- [ ] Payment processing set up (Stripe, Square, etc.)
- [ ] Portfolio/website current
- [ ] Review profiles active (Google Business, Yelp, etc.)
- [ ] Social media presence maintained
- [ ] Professional headshot and action photos available
- [ ] Bio/about text written

---

> **Usage Note:** This blueprint contains every possible checkpoint across the full spectrum of private chef engagements. Most dinners will use a subset. A casual dinner for 4 might touch 30 checkpoints. A 50-person corporate event with NDAs, staff, rentals, and vendor coordination might touch 150+. The system should let chefs activate or deactivate checkpoints based on their business style, and per-event complexity should auto-suggest which checkpoints are relevant.
