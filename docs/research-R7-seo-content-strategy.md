## Executive Summary

Food service discovery is a fragmented SEO landscape with enormous opportunity for a platform willing to invest in programmatic, localized content. The private chef and catering niches are dominated by a handful of directory platforms (Thumbtack, Bark, Yelp) and individual chef websites, but no single platform owns the space the way The Knot owns wedding planning or DoorDash owns food delivery search.

The core opportunity: "private chef" gets approximately 4,600 monthly searches in the US, "personal chef" gets comparable volume, and the long-tail variations (city + service, dietary + service, cost + service) collectively represent tens of thousands of monthly searches with moderate to low keyword difficulty. The highest-value content play is cost/pricing guides ("How much does a private chef cost?" and its variations), which attract high-intent searchers ready to book.

Meanwhile, AI search is reshaping discovery fundamentally. Google AI Overviews now appear in 25% of searches and have caused a 58-61% drop in organic CTR for affected queries. But AI referral traffic converts at 4.4x the rate of organic search, making visibility in AI systems disproportionately valuable. Food and restaurant queries saw a 387% increase in AI Overview appearances in early 2025, signaling that this vertical is a priority for Google's AI integration.

ChefFlow's strategic advantage: by building a platform with structured chef profiles, verified reviews, transparent pricing, and dietary constraint data, ChefFlow can become the authoritative data source that both traditional search engines and AI systems cite when users ask about private chefs and food services.

Key findings:

1. **The private chef SEO niche is winnable.** No dominant platform exists. Thumbtack ranks for broad terms but lacks depth. Individual chef blogs rank for cost queries. A platform with programmatic city + service pages can capture this traffic systematically.
2. **Dietary-specific food service searches are almost entirely unowned.** Nobody dominates "gluten-free caterer in [city]" or "vegan private chef near me." This is ChefFlow's content blue ocean.
3. **Programmatic SEO at scale is the proven playbook.** Thumbtack generates 6.47M monthly visits with 200K+ first-page rankings, primarily through auto-generated city + service pages. This model is directly applicable to food services.
4. **AI search optimization (GEO) is the next frontier.** Platforms that structure data well, accumulate reviews, and produce authoritative content today will be the ones AI recommends for years to come.
5. **Social media drives awareness but not bookings.** TikTok and Instagram are powerful for chef brand-building (55% of users visit a restaurant after TikTok discovery), but the conversion funnel from social to booking requires a platform intermediary, which is exactly what ChefFlow provides.

## 2. How Competitors Win SEO

### 2.1 Thumbtack: The Programmatic SEO Masterclass

Thumbtack is the clearest model for what ChefFlow's SEO strategy should become.

**Traffic profile:**

- ~6.47M monthly visits (SimilarWeb, September 2025)
- Organic search accounts for ~22% of total traffic (~1.4M organic visits/month)
- 200K+ keywords ranking on Google's first page
- Covers ~500 service categories across thousands of cities

**How they do it:**
Thumbtack generates a unique page for every combination of [service] + [city]. For example: "Personal Chefs in Miami," "Wedding Caterers in Denver," "Corporate Catering in Austin." Each page pulls from a database template with:

- Local provider listings with ratings and reviews
- Average pricing for that service in that city
- FAQ section with location-specific answers
- Schema markup for local business listings
- Internal links to related services and nearby cities

**What makes it work:**

- Each page has unique content (provider names, reviews, prices vary by city)
- Strong internal linking between cities and service categories
- High domain authority (years of link building, PR, content marketing)
- User-generated content (reviews, Q&A) adds unique text to each page

**Traffic acquisition cost:** Programmatic SEO costs 30-70% less than equivalent paid search campaigns over a 12-month period. Once pages are indexed and ranking, the marginal cost of maintaining them is near zero.

(Sources: [SimilarWeb](https://www.similarweb.com/website/thumbtack.com/), [SEMrush](https://www.semrush.com/website/thumbtack.com/overview/), [LeadCapture.io](https://leadcapture.io/blog/thumbtacks-lead-generation-strategy/), [SeoProfy Programmatic SEO](https://seoprofy.com/blog/programmatic-seo/))

### 2.2 Yelp: Review-Powered Local Dominance

Yelp dominates local food searches through a different mechanism: review volume and domain authority.

**How Yelp wins:**

- Yelp pages consistently rank in Google's top 3 for "[restaurant/service] near me" queries
- Nearly 2.5 million daily users searching for local businesses
- Review accumulation creates unique, constantly refreshing content that Google values
- Strong structured data implementation (schema markup for business listings)
- Apple Maps integration extends Yelp's reach to iOS users
- Yelp is increasingly used as a structured data source for AI systems

**Yelp ranking factors (internal algorithm):**

- Review quality and authenticity
- Review recency and consistency
- Reviewer credibility (established accounts weighted higher)
- Profile completeness (hours, categories, photos, menu)
- User engagement signals (clicks, calls, direction requests)

**Revenue impact:** Claiming a Yelp business page can increase revenue by 5-10%. Yelp's dominance comes from the virtuous cycle: more reviews attract more users, more users generate more reviews.

(Sources: [Rezku Blog](https://rezku.com/blog/yelp-local-seo-for-restaurants/), [Chatmeter](https://www.chatmeter.com/resource/blog/yelp-seo/), [LocalMighty](https://www.localmighty.com/blog/yelp-for-business/))

### 2.3 The Knot / WeddingWire: Owning the Wedding Vertical

Both platforms (owned by The Knot Worldwide) dominate wedding catering searches through vertical specialization and paid vendor prominence.

**How they win:**

- Years of domain authority built through wedding-focused content
- Vendor profiles with reviews sorted by rating, creating trust signals
- Heavy investment in both organic SEO and Google AdWords
- Category pages for every wedding vendor type in every metro area
- WeddingWire listings frequently appear above individual vendor websites for "[city] wedding venue/caterer" searches

**The pay-to-play model:**
Vendor visibility on both platforms is heavily influenced by paid listings. Free listings exist but rank poorly. This creates a revenue model but also a vulnerability: vendors who feel they're paying for visibility they should earn organically often leave the platform.

**Relevance to ChefFlow:** Wedding catering is a high-value vertical ($5,000-$25,000+ per event) but is heavily locked up by The Knot/WeddingWire. ChefFlow's opportunity is in the spaces these platforms ignore: intimate dinner parties, dietary-specific events, ongoing personal chef services, corporate catering, and non-wedding celebrations.

(Sources: [Johnson Jones Group](https://johnsonjonesgroup.com/weddingwire-vs-the-knot-we-asked-the-vendors/), [Nuphoriq](https://nuphoriq.com/the-knot-vs-weddingwire/), [FullyBookedVenue](https://www.fullybookedvenue.com/wedding-wire-vs-the-knot/))

### 2.4 DoorDash / UberEats: Menu Pages at Scale

Food delivery platforms have built massive SEO footprints through restaurant and menu pages.

**How they do it:**

- Every restaurant on the platform gets its own SEO-optimized page with menu, hours, ratings, and photos
- Menu items are rendered as HTML text (not PDFs), making them crawlable and indexable
- Category pages for cuisine types in each city ("Italian restaurants in Chicago")
- Strong domain authority from billions of backlinks and brand recognition
- Structured data for menus, pricing, and availability

**Key stat:** 68% of mobile food orders now start directly in delivery apps, meaning these platforms are becoming search engines themselves, not just search results.

**Relevance to ChefFlow:** ChefFlow can adopt the same approach for chef profiles. Each chef on the platform gets a richly structured page with their specialties, pricing, dietary expertise, service areas, reviews, and photos. These pages become SEO assets that rank for "[chef name] + [city]" and "[service type] + [city]" queries.

(Sources: [DoorDash Merchant Blog](https://merchants.doordash.com/en-us/blog/restaurant-seo), [SerpGrowth](https://serpsgrowth.com/blog/seo-strategies-for-food-delivery-services/))

## 3. Content Strategy for Food Platforms

### 3.1 Content Types That Drive Food Service Traffic

Based on analysis of what ranks for food service queries, the highest-performing content types are:

**Tier 1: Highest Traffic, Highest Intent**

1. **Cost/pricing guides** - "How much does a private chef cost?" is the single highest-value content piece in this niche. It attracts searchers who are actively considering hiring and need pricing context to make a decision. Individual chef blogs (Care.com, Jason Raffin, GastroLove, Down to Earth Cuisine) currently rank for these queries because no platform has built a definitive, data-backed pricing guide.

2. **City + service landing pages** - Programmatic pages like "Personal Chefs in Austin" with actual provider listings, pricing ranges, and reviews. These capture the highest-intent local searches.

**Tier 2: High Traffic, Educational Intent** 3. **Comparison guides** - "Personal chef vs. meal delivery: which is right for you?" or "Private chef vs. catering: what's the difference?" These educate and guide searchers toward the right service.

4. **How-to guides** - "How to hire a private chef for a dinner party" or "What to expect from a personal chef service." These build trust and establish platform authority.

**Tier 3: Supporting Content** 5. **Event planning guides** - "Planning a 50th birthday dinner with a private chef" or "How to organize corporate team lunches." Seasonal and event-driven traffic.

6. **Dietary guides** - "Finding a nut-free caterer: what to look for" or "Questions to ask a vegan personal chef." Serves an underserved audience with high willingness to pay.

### 3.2 "How Much Does a Private Chef Cost?" - The #1 Content Opportunity

This single query (and its variations) represents the biggest content opportunity in the private chef space. Here is why:

**Current ranking landscape:**
The top results for "how much does a private chef cost" are dominated by individual chef websites and generic content sites, not platforms. The current #1 results include:

- Care.com (generic guide, no real data)
- Individual chef blogs (Jason Raffin, GastroLove, Down to Earth Cuisine, Personal Chef Ana)
- City-specific chef services (Charleston Chef Service, Atlanta Personal Chef Service)

**What a definitive guide should include:**

- Real pricing data aggregated from platform chefs (not estimates)
- Breakdown by service type (dinner party, weekly meal prep, full-time, special event)
- Breakdown by city/region (NYC vs. Austin vs. rural areas)
- Breakdown by dietary specialization (standard vs. allergen-free vs. specialized cuisine)
- Calculator tool (input: guests, city, service type, output: estimated price range)
- Comparison with alternatives (meal kits, restaurant catering, grocery delivery)

**Why ChefFlow wins this:** A platform with real chef pricing data can build the most authoritative cost guide on the internet. Individual chefs can only share their own rates. ChefFlow can share aggregated, anonymized data across hundreds of chefs, making the guide genuinely more useful than anything else that exists.

(Sources: [Care.com](https://www.care.com/c/how-much-does-a-personal-chef-cost/), [Jason Raffin](https://www.jasonraffin.com/post/how-much-is-a-private-chef), [GastroLove](https://www.gastrolovellc.com/how-much-does-a-private-chef-cost), [Personal Chef Ana](https://personalchefana.com/faq/private-chef-cost-guide/))

### 3.3 Educational vs. Transactional Content

**Educational content** (blog posts, guides, how-tos) drives top-of-funnel traffic but has lower conversion rates. Its primary value is building domain authority, earning backlinks, and establishing trust.

**Transactional content** (city + service pages, chef profiles, pricing pages, booking flows) converts at much higher rates but requires supply-side data (actual chefs, real pricing, genuine reviews).

**The funnel:**

1. Searcher finds educational content ("How much does a private chef cost?")
2. Content includes internal links to city-specific pages ("Find private chefs in your area")
3. City page shows actual chef profiles with pricing, reviews, and booking buttons
4. Searcher books through the platform

**Conversion benchmarks:**

- Blog/guide content: 1-3% click-through to transactional pages
- City + service landing pages: 3-8% conversion to inquiry/booking
- Chef profile pages (from direct search): 8-15% conversion to inquiry

### 3.4 User-Generated Content as SEO Fuel

Reviews, Q&A, and chef-written content are the most scalable source of unique text on a platform.

**Why UGC matters for SEO:**

- Every review adds unique, keyword-rich text to a page
- Reviews naturally include location names, service types, and dietary terms
- Fresh content signals to Google that the page is active and relevant
- Q&A sections capture long-tail queries that no one would write content for manually

**Customer photos drive 4x higher conversion** than branded/professional photos, making user-submitted event photos valuable for both SEO and conversion.

(Sources: [Restroworks](https://www.restroworks.com/blog/restaurant-social-media-statistics/), [CropInk](https://cropink.com/restaurant-social-media-statistics))

## 4. Local SEO for Food Services

### 4.1 Google Business Profile Optimization

The Google Business Profile (GBP) is the single most important ranking factor for local food service searches, accounting for approximately 32% of all map pack ranking factors.

**For individual chefs on ChefFlow:**

- Each chef should have their own GBP listing as a Service Area Business (SAB)
- Category should be the most specific available (e.g., "Personal Chef Service" rather than "Restaurant")
- Service areas should be defined by the cities/neighborhoods they serve
- Menu of services should be listed with pricing ranges
- Photos of actual food and events should be uploaded regularly
- Reviews should be actively solicited after every service

**For ChefFlow as a platform:**

- ChefFlow itself can maintain a GBP as a "Food Service" or "Event Planning" company
- But the real value is in helping each chef on the platform optimize their own GBP
- A "GBP optimization guide" for onboarding chefs would both help chefs and improve the platform's overall search footprint

### 4.2 Local Pack Ranking Factors for Food

Google's local pack (the map with 3 business listings) is the most valuable search real estate for food services. The three primary factors:

1. **Relevance** (how well the business matches the search query)
   - Use specific categories: "Personal Chef Service," "Catering Service," "Event Caterer"
   - Include detailed service descriptions with relevant keywords
   - List dietary specializations explicitly

2. **Distance** (proximity to the searcher)
   - Define service areas accurately
   - For platforms without physical locations: focus on service area designation

3. **Prominence** (how well-known and trusted the business is)
   - Review volume and average rating are the strongest signals
   - Review signals account for 15%+ of local pack ranking
   - NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency across all directories
   - Mentions in local media, blogs, and community sites

(Sources: [Colling Media](https://collingmedia.com/search-engine-optimization-seo/google-my-business-local-ranking-factors/), [LocalDominator](https://localdominator.co/local-search-ranking-factors/), [OnTheMap Agency](https://onthemap.agency/blog/google-business-profile-restaurants/))

### 4.3 How Platforms Rank Locally Without Physical Locations

This is a critical challenge for ChefFlow. The platform doesn't have a physical storefront in every city, yet needs to rank for "[service] in [city]" queries.

**Strategies that work:**

1. **Service Area Business (SAB) designation** on GBP, specifying covered metro areas without a physical address
2. **City-specific landing pages** with unique, localized content (not just template text with the city name swapped). Include local chef profiles, local pricing data, and testimonials from local clients
3. **Local directory citations** on platforms that allow service-area businesses (Yelp, BBB, industry directories)
4. **Community involvement signals** - partnerships with local food banks, culinary schools, or chef associations in target cities
5. **Local backlinks** from city-specific food blogs, lifestyle publications, and event planning sites

**The Thumbtack model applied:** Thumbtack has no physical presence in most cities but ranks for thousands of "[service] in [city]" queries through programmatic city pages backed by real provider listings and reviews. ChefFlow can replicate this once it has chef supply in target metros.

(Sources: [Dallas SEO Company](https://www.thedallasseocompany.com/blog/local-seo-with-no-physical-locations), [MarketDing](https://marketding.com/blog/how-to-rank-for-nearby-cities-without-physical-locations), [Creative Click Media](https://creativeclickmedia.com/dominate-local-seo/))

## 5. AI Search and the Future of Food Discovery

### 5.1 The Current State of AI Search (2025-2026)

AI is fundamentally reshaping how people discover food services. The data is dramatic:

**AI Overview penetration:**

- AI Overviews appear in 25% of all Google searches (Conductor, November 2025)
- Restaurant/food queries saw a 387% increase in AI Overview appearances in early 2025

- 43% of AI Overview searches result in zero clicks (SEMrush, September 2025)

**CTR impact:**

- Organic CTR dropped 58-61% year-over-year for queries with AI Overviews (Ahrefs/Seer Interactive)
- Traditional CTR drops from 15% to 8% when AI Overview is present (Pew Research Center, July 2025)
- Only 1% of searches lead to users clicking links within AI Overviews (Pew Research Center)
- But brands cited in AI Overviews earn 35% more organic clicks and 91% more paid clicks (Seer Interactive)

**AI platform usage:**

- ChatGPT: 800 million weekly users processing 2.5 billion prompts daily (August 2025)
- 31% of ChatGPT prompts trigger web search (Nectiv, October 2025)
- ChatGPT usage for general information tripled from 4.1% to 12.5% (February to August 2025)
- ChatGPT holds 80.49% AI chatbot market share
- 87.4% of AI referral traffic comes from ChatGPT (Conductor)

(Sources: [Position Digital](https://www.position.digital/blog/ai-seo-statistics/), [Exposure Ninja](https://exposureninja.com/blog/ai-search-statistics/), [DataSlayer](https://www.dataslayer.ai/blog/google-ai-overviews-the-end-of-traditional-ctr-and-how-to-adapt-in-2025))

### 5.3 How to Optimize for AI Search (GEO - Generative Engine Optimization)

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of positioning content so AI platforms cite, recommend, or mention your brand.

**Platform-specific citation preferences:**

| AI Platform | Primary Citation Sources                    | Strategy                                                                  |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| ChatGPT     | Wikipedia (47.9%), established publications | Build Wikipedia presence, get cited in major food/lifestyle publications  |
| Perplexity  | Reddit (46.7%), community content           | Participate authentically in r/personalchefs, r/catering, food subreddits |
| Google AI   | YouTube, LinkedIn, professional sources     | Create YouTube content, maintain active LinkedIn presence                 |
| Copilot     | Forbes, business publications               | Target business media coverage                                            |

**Key insight:** Only 11% of domain citations overlap between ChatGPT and Perplexity, meaning 89% of citation opportunities require platform-specific strategies.

**Six GEO principles for ChefFlow:**

1. **Structure data for machine readability.** Implement comprehensive schema markup. Make every chef profile, every pricing page, and every city page parseable by AI crawlers.

2. **Build entity signals.** Maintain consistent brand mentions across the web. Create detailed About pages and chef bio pages. Pursue a Wikipedia presence when the platform reaches sufficient notability.

3. **Create self-contained content blocks.** Each section of content should be understandable without reading the rest of the page. AI systems extract fragments, not full articles.

4. **Accumulate authentic reviews.** AI systems weight human-generated content heavily, especially in food and hospitality. Real customer reviews mentioning specific dishes, dietary accommodations, and experiences are gold for AI citation.

5. **Participate in community platforms.** Reddit is Perplexity's #1 citation source. Authentic participation in food service subreddits builds citation potential.

6. **Publish authoritative data.** Original research, pricing surveys, industry reports, and trend analyses are cited by AI systems because they provide unique information unavailable elsewhere.

(Sources: [SearchEngineLand GEO Guide](https://searchengineland.com/what-is-generative-engine-optimization-geo-444418), [Backlinko GEO](https://backlinko.com/generative-engine-optimization-geo), [Malou.io AI for Restaurants](https://www.malou.io/en-us/blog/restaurants-ai-chatgpt), [GetPassionfruit GEO Guide](https://www.getpassionfruit.com/blog/generative-engine-optimization-guide-for-chatgpt-perplexity-gemini-claude-copilot))

### 5.4 The Zero-Click Threat and Opportunity

**The threat:** When Google AI Overviews answer "How much does a private chef cost?" directly in the search results, users may never click through to ChefFlow's cost guide. Zero-click searches already account for 60-65% of all searches.

**The opportunity:** If ChefFlow is the source cited by the AI Overview, the brand still gets visibility, trust, and association with authoritative pricing data, even without the click. Brands cited in AI Overviews see 35% higher CTR on their organic results below the overview.

**The strategic response:**

- Accept that informational queries will increasingly be answered without clicks
- Focus on being the cited source in AI answers (brand visibility without the click)
- Drive transactional queries to platform pages where AI can't fully answer (booking, browsing chef profiles, comparing options)
- Build direct traffic through brand recognition earned from AI citations

### 6.2 Discovery vs. Conversion: The Gap

The critical distinction: social media drives discovery and awareness, but conversion to booking requires a platform intermediary.

- Social media drives a 9.9% lift in B2C revenue for restaurants with strategic efforts

**The gap ChefFlow fills:**
A chef's TikTok video goes viral (awareness). The viewer wants to hire them (intent). Where do they go? Currently: the chef's personal website (if it exists), a DM on Instagram, or a Google search that leads to Thumbtack. ChefFlow can be the destination that converts social media awareness into booked services.

**Content strategy for social:**

- ChefFlow provides the link-in-bio destination, the booking page, the pricing transparency
- ChefFlow aggregates chef content on city pages for SEO benefit
- Platform-generated content (cost guides, dietary guides, event planning tips) drives search traffic that social cannot

## 7. ChefFlow's Content Strategy

### 7.1 The Programmatic SEO Play

**Phase 1: Template infrastructure (before marketplace launch)**
Build the page templates and URL structure for:

- `/chefs/[city]` - "Private Chefs in [City]"
- `/catering/[city]` - "Catering Services in [City]"
- `/[dietary]/[city]` - "Gluten-Free Caterers in [City]"
- `/[service]/[city]` - "Wedding Caterers in [City]"

**Phase 2: Seed content (with early chef supply)**

- Populate pages with real chef profiles as chefs join
- Add pricing data from platform chefs (aggregated, anonymized)
- Include FAQ sections with city-specific information
- Implement full schema markup on every page

**Phase 3: Scale (post-launch)**

- Auto-generate pages for every city where ChefFlow has 3+ chefs
- Build internal linking between related cities and service types
- Accumulate reviews and UGC that add unique content to each page
- Target long-tail keywords that no one else is serving

**Estimated page count at scale:**

- 200 US metro areas x 8 service types = 1,600 city + service pages
- 200 metros x 6 dietary types = 1,200 dietary + city pages
- 200 metros x 1 cost guide per metro = 200 localized pricing pages
- Individual chef profiles: 1,000-10,000 pages
- Total: 4,000-13,000+ unique, indexable pages

### 7.2 Chef Profiles as SEO Assets

Every chef profile on ChefFlow should be a standalone SEO asset that ranks for:

- "[Chef Name]" (branded search)
- "[Chef Name] + [city]" (local branded search)
- "[Service type] + [city]" (generic local search, via chef profile appearing on city pages)

**What each chef profile should include (for SEO):**

- Full name, city, service area (H1, meta title, schema)
- Specialties and cuisine types (keyword-rich content)
- Dietary expertise (allergen-free, vegan, kosher, halal, etc.)
- Pricing ranges (answers "how much" queries)
- Reviews with star ratings (schema markup for AggregateRating)
- Photos of actual food and events (image SEO, visual engagement)
- FAQ section (captures long-tail queries)
- Booking/inquiry call-to-action (conversion)

### 7.3 Cost Guides and Pricing Transparency

**The content play:**

- One master guide: "How Much Does a Private Chef Cost in 2026?"
- City-specific variants: "Private Chef Prices in [City]" for top 50 metros
- Service-specific variants: "How Much Does Wedding Catering Cost?" / "Corporate Catering Pricing Guide"
- Dietary-specific variants: "Cost of a Gluten-Free Personal Chef"

**Why pricing transparency wins:**

- Cost queries have the highest commercial intent in this niche
- Nobody else has real, aggregated pricing data (individual chefs only know their own rates)
- Pricing transparency builds trust and establishes ChefFlow as the authoritative source
- These pages naturally attract backlinks from financial planning, lifestyle, and food media sites

### 7.4 The Dietary Constraint Content Angle

This is ChefFlow's unique content moat. No platform currently owns the intersection of dietary restrictions and chef/catering services.

**Content plan:**

- "Finding a Nut-Free Caterer: The Complete Safety Guide" (targets allergy parents)
- "Hiring a Vegan Personal Chef: What to Expect" (targets growing vegan market)
- "Gluten-Free Catering for Corporate Events" (targets corporate event planners)
- "Kosher Catering in [City]: Your Complete Guide" (targets Jewish communities)
- "Halal Catering in [City]: Finding Certified Providers" (targets Muslim communities)
- "Keto-Friendly Personal Chefs: How Meal Prep Services Work" (targets keto dieters)
- "AIP Diet Chef: Finding a Chef Who Understands Autoimmune Protocol" (ultra-niche, zero competition)

**Each guide should include:**

- What to look for in a chef/caterer for this dietary need
- Questions to ask before hiring
- Red flags and safety considerations (especially for allergies)
- Average pricing for dietary-specialized services
- Link to ChefFlow's filtered search for that dietary type in the reader's city

### 7.5 Content-to-Booking Funnel

The complete funnel from content discovery to marketplace transaction:

```
1. AWARENESS (Top of Funnel)
   Content: Blog posts, social media, cost guides
   Goal: Get found, build trust
   Metric: Organic traffic, time on page

2. CONSIDERATION (Middle of Funnel)
   Content: City + service pages, comparison guides, dietary guides
   Goal: Show real options, demonstrate platform value
   Metric: Click-through to chef profiles

3. EVALUATION (Bottom of Funnel)
   Content: Chef profiles, reviews, pricing pages
   Goal: Build confidence in specific chef
   Metric: Inquiry/booking rate

4. BOOKING (Conversion)
   Content: Booking flow, payment processing
   Goal: Complete transaction
   Metric: Conversion rate, booking value

5. RETENTION (Post-Booking)
   Content: Review prompts, follow-up guides, loyalty features
   Goal: Generate reviews (SEO fuel) and repeat bookings
   Metric: Review rate, repeat booking rate
```

**The flywheel:** More chefs join the platform, creating more city pages, attracting more organic traffic, generating more bookings, producing more reviews, which improves SEO rankings, which attracts more traffic. Each cycle makes the next one stronger.
