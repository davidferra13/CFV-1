> "You assign it. They see it instantly."
> Duration: 60 seconds | Format: 1920x1080 (YouTube 16:9) | Auth: chef + staff (dual context)

## The pitch in one sentence

The chef assigns staff to an event with station assignments, task lists, and timing. The video cuts to the staff portal where the assignment appears instantly with everything they need: tasks, station, time, checklist. The chef sees progress updates in real time.

## A-HA MOMENTS (3 total)

1. **Both sides:** Chef assigns, staff sees it instantly. No phone calls, no text chains, no "did you get my message?"
2. **Intelligence:** Dietary alerts flow to the staff automatically. The sous chef sees nut allergy warnings without the chef having to remember to tell them.
3. **Scale:** Real-time progress tracking. The chef sees task completion from their team without hovering over shoulders.

## CAPTION COUNT: 10 total

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- **Dual context:** Chef auth + Staff auth. Need a staff account in the system with the staff portal accessible.
- Staff portal pages must be functional: assignment detail, task checklist, time clock. If any of these aren't built, simulate with pre-seeded data and manual navigation.
- The task checkbox interaction (staff checks off a task, chef sees it update) is the key "real-time" moment. If real-time updates aren't implemented, fake it with a page refresh on the chef side or pre-seed the state.
- Dietary alerts on the staff assignment page are critical. If they're not showing, check that event guest dietary data flows to the staff view.
- Time clock scene (Scene 5) is quick (5 seconds). If the time clock feature isn't built, cut this scene and extend Scene 4 instead.
- This video appeals especially to caterers and restaurant owners who manage teams. It's also relevant to private chefs who occasionally hire sous chefs or servers for larger events.
