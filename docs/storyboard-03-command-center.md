# Storyboard #3: The Command Center (Dashboard)

> "Open your laptop. Know exactly what to do."
> Duration: 60 seconds | Format: 1920x1080 (YouTube 16:9) | Auth: chef account

---

## The pitch in one sentence

The dashboard is the first thing a chef sees every morning. If it looks powerful and immediately useful, they stay. If it looks empty or confusing, they churn. This video proves ChefFlow replaces the morning routine of checking 5 apps, 3 inboxes, and a spreadsheet.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- The dashboard must have rich, realistic data. Seed the agent account with: 8-10 events across different states, 15+ clients, payment history, at least 2 overdue follow-ups, recent revenue.
- The health score should show a "good but not perfect" number (75-85 range). A 100 looks fake. A 50 looks bad. 78 is believable and inviting.
- The week strip should show a mix: 2-3 event days, 1 prep day, 2-3 free days. A completely full week looks unsustainable. A completely empty week looks unused.
- Priority queue should have exactly 5-6 items. Fewer looks undercooked. More looks overwhelming.
- The Cmd+K scene is fast (5 seconds). Practice the typing speed. The command palette must respond instantly (no loading spinners).
- Record on beta for instant page loads. The dashboard has many widgets that load asynchronously; localhost compile delays will create visible loading states that ruin the polished feel.
- This video is a scroll-heavy tour. Every scroll must be smooth and deliberate. No jerky movements. Practice the scroll distances.
