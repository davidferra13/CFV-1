## §2. Definitions

- **Chef**: An independent culinary professional who creates a ChefFlow account to manage their private chef or catering business.
- **Client**: A person who creates a ChefFlow account (via Chef invitation or standalone signup) and accesses the Client Portal.
- **Event**: A culinary service booking (dinner party, meal prep service, private dining, catered event, etc.) tracked in ChefFlow.
- **Inquiry**: A request for culinary services submitted by a prospective Client through a Chef's public page, or entered by a Chef on behalf of a Client.
- **Quote**: A formal service proposal sent from a Chef to a Client through ChefFlow.
- **Platform Fee**: The percentage fee ChefFlow deducts from Stripe-processed client payments before disbursing the remainder to the Chef. The current rate is disclosed in the Chef's account settings and at Stripe Connect onboarding.
- **Stripe Connect**: The Stripe payment infrastructure used to process client-to-chef payments through ChefFlow.
- **Client Portal**: The authenticated interface at `/my-events`, `/my-quotes`, `/my-chat`, and related pages where Clients view events, approve Quotes, and make payments.
- **Public Form**: The unauthenticated inquiry submission form at a Chef's public profile page.
- **Local AI**: ChefFlow's on-device AI (Ollama) that processes private data on the Chef's own machine without transmitting it to external servers.
- **Manual Payment**: A payment recorded in ChefFlow but processed outside Stripe (cash, Venmo, Zelle, PayPal, check).

## §7. Payments, Fees & Refunds

### 7.1 How Payments Work

Client payments for Events are processed through Stripe Connect. ChefFlow acts as the payment collection agent for the Chef; receipt by ChefFlow satisfies the Client's payment obligation for that transaction.

From each Stripe-processed payment, ChefFlow deducts:

1. The **Platform Fee** (percentage; disclosed at onboarding and in account settings)
2. **Stripe processing fees** (typically 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction in the US, or equivalent per local Stripe pricing)

The net amount is disbursed to the Chef's connected Stripe account on Stripe's standard payout schedule.

### 7.2 Payment Plans

Chefs may structure Events with deposit, installment, and final payment schedules. The schedule is set by the Chef in the Quote and accepted by the Client. ChefFlow sends automated payment reminders per the Chef's configured schedule.

If a Client misses a scheduled payment, ChefFlow may place the Event on hold until payment is received.

### 7.3 Manual Payments

Chefs may record Manual Payments (cash, Venmo, Zelle, PayPal, check) in ChefFlow's ledger. ChefFlow does not process, hold, verify, or dispute Manual Payments. Disputes involving Manual Payments are solely between the Chef and Client.

### 7.4 Gift Cards

Gift card purchases are processed via Stripe Checkout. Gift card balances are non-refundable credits against future Events and have no cash value. Gift cards expire per the expiry date set by the issuing Chef, which must be disclosed at the time of purchase.

### 7.5 Refunds

Refunds for Event cancellations are governed by the Chef's published cancellation policy, not by ChefFlow. ChefFlow does not set universal cancellation terms.

If the Chef approves a refund or a dispute is resolved in the Client's favor:

- ChefFlow will process the Stripe reversal within 5–10 business days
- Stripe processing fees are non-refundable
- ChefFlow's Platform Fee may be refunded at ChefFlow's sole discretion

### 7.6 Platform Fee Changes

- [ ] **§1** — Legal entity name and state of incorporation
- [ ] **§4** — Adequacy of "not a party to" language for your specific payment flow
- [ ] **§5.2** — Whether to require (not merely recommend) liability insurance
- [ ] **§7.1** — Exact Platform Fee percentage to disclose
- [ ] **§9.3** — JAMS vs. AAA, arbitration location, and fee allocation
- [ ] **§9.4** — Confirm governing state
- [ ] **§11 & §12** — Confirm all-caps disclaimer format meets enforceability standard in governing state
- [ ] **§15 (GDPR)** — Whether a formal DPA and EU representative are required given user scale
- [ ] **§15 (CCPA)** — Confirm California-specific language meets current CPRA requirements
- [ ] Entire document — Compliance with any new state-level consumer protection laws (Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, Connecticut CTDPA, etc.)
