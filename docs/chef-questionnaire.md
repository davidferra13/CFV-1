# ChefFlow — Private Chef Survey

**Thank you for taking the time to fill this out.** Your answers will directly shape the tools we're building for private chefs. There are no right or wrong answers — we want to understand how you actually work, not how we assume you work.

This survey has 10 sections and takes approximately 25–35 minutes to complete thoughtfully. The open-ended sections are the most valuable — please don't hold back.

---

## Section 1 — Your Operation

1. How many events do you do per month on average?

2. Do you work solo, or do you bring staff? If you bring staff, how many typically?

3. What types of events do you primarily do? _(check all that apply)_
   - [ ] Private dinner parties (small, 2–8 guests)
   - [ ] Private dinner parties (larger, 9–20 guests)
   - [ ] Full-service catered events (20+ guests)
   - [ ] Meal prep / weekly cook
   - [ ] Corporate events
   - [ ] Weddings or large milestones
   - [ ] Other: **\*\***\_\_\_**\*\***

4. What's your typical event size in number of guests?

5. What geographic area do you primarily serve?

---

## Section 2 — Current Tools & Process

6. What tools, apps, or software do you currently use to run your business? _(list everything — even if it's just "my phone" or "a spreadsheet")_

7. How do you currently send quotes or proposals to clients?

8. How do you currently track deposits and payments?

9. How do you manage your recipes and menus? _(written notebook, Google Docs, your head, an app, etc.)_

10. What is the single most frustrating administrative task in your business right now?

---

## Section 3 — Getting Clients & Confirming Bookings

11. How do most new clients find you?

12. When a new inquiry comes in, what's your goal for response time?

13. Walk me through how you go from first contact with a new client to a confirmed, locked-in booking. What are all the steps, in order?

14. Do clients typically request changes after they receive a proposal? How often, and what kind of changes?

15. Do you require a deposit to hold a date?
    - If yes: What percentage, and when do you collect it?
    - If yes: What happens if a client cancels after paying a deposit?

16. Have you ever lost a booking because your response or proposal process felt too slow or complicated? Tell me about it if so.

---

## Section 4 — Menus & Pricing

17. How do you build a menu for a new client — do you start from scratch each time, use templates, or something in between?

18. How do you price your events? _(per person, flat rate, hourly, or a mix?)_

19. Do you include the cost of groceries in your pricing, or do groceries get handled separately?

20. How do you handle dietary restrictions or customization requests?

21. At what point in the process do clients typically see a written menu? Is it before or after they've confirmed/paid?

22. How often do you modify the menu on the actual day of service? What causes those changes?

23. Do you keep a personal library of your recipes and dishes, or is most of it stored in your head?

---

## Section 5 — Grocery Shopping

24. How do you currently build your shopping list for an event?

25. Do you shop yourself, or do you delegate? If you delegate, who handles it?

26. Do you shop at one store or multiple? Which stores do you use most often?

27. Do you currently keep your receipts? If so, how do you track them?

28. Have you ever had leftover ingredients from one event that you used at a future event? Is this common for you?

29. Would a tool that automatically generates a shopping list directly from your planned menu be something you'd actually use?

---

## Section 6 — Day-of Service _(Most Important Section)_

30. Walk me through a typical **prep day** — what does it look like start to finish, in chronological order?

31. Walk me through a typical **service day** — what's the order of operations from the moment you wake up until you leave the client's home?

32. Do you do a walkthrough of the client's kitchen before the event? What are you checking for?

33. What is the most common thing that goes wrong on a service day?

34. Do you currently use any written or printed checklists during a service? Would you?

35. Do you leave any kind of post-event note or summary for the client? What does that look like?

36. What does cleanup look like? Who does it, what's the standard you hold yourself to, and when does it happen in relation to the meal?

---

## Section 7 — Client Communication

37. What's your primary way of communicating with clients — text, email, phone calls, or something else?

38. How many back-and-forth exchanges do you typically have with a client between the time they book and the day of service?

39. Have clients ever contacted you during a service for non-urgent things? How do you handle that?

40. Do you send any kind of follow-up message after a service is complete? What does it say?

41. Have you ever had a client dispute or complaint? How did you handle it?

---

## Section 8 — Financials & Getting Paid

42. When do you collect the remaining balance after a deposit?
    - [ ] At least a week before the event
    - [ ] 1–2 days before the event
    - [ ] Day of the event (before service)
    - [ ] Day of the event (after service)
    - [ ] After the event (invoice sent later)
    - [ ] Other: **\*\***\_\_\_**\*\***

43. Do you use formal invoices, or is it more informal (Venmo, Zelle, cash, etc.)?

44. Do you add a gratuity automatically, or is it left to the client's discretion?

45. How do you track your business expenses? (receipts, spreadsheet, accounting software, etc.)

46. Do you have a general sense of your profit margin per event — meaning after groceries, labor, and time, what do you actually make?

47. Have you ever had a client not pay, or pay significantly late? How did you handle it?

---

## Section 9 — Staff & Assistants

_(If you always work solo, you can skip this section.)_

48. When you bring on help for an event, how do you find and hire them?

49. How do you communicate event details to your staff? (send them a document, call them, brief them in person, etc.)

50. How do you pay your staff — same day or later? Cash or transfer?

51. What information does your staff absolutely need to have before they show up to an event?

---

## Section 10 — The Big Picture _(Please don't skip this section)_

52. If you had a magic wand and could instantly fix or add one thing to how you run your business — what would it be?

53. What part of running your private chef business feels the most out of control or disorganized right now?

54. Is there anything about the way private chefs actually work that you think outsiders — or software developers — consistently get wrong or misunderstand?

---

_Thank you — this is incredibly valuable. Every answer here will be used to make the platform better for working chefs._
