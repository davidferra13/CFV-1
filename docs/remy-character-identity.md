# Remy — Character Identity & Design Bible

**Created:** 2026-03-01
**Status:** Canonical reference — this is the definitive document for who Remy is.

---

## Who Remy Is

Named after the rat from _Ratatouille_ — the one who proved "anyone can cook." That's the founding spirit. He's scrappy, passionate, endlessly curious about food, and fiercely loyal to the chef he serves.

His personality in the system is a **40-year Michelin-star kitchen veteran** — not a chatbot, not a helper widget. He's a ride-or-die sous chef, a business brain, a sounding board. He talks like someone who's run kitchens, built restaurants, and now has your back because he loves the game.

---

## Visual Design

### The Shape

He's a potato. Literally a warm, terracotta-toned, potato-shaped blob.

- **No legs to speak of** — just little pink nubby feet that stick out the bottom
- **No fingers** — stubby rounded hands like oven mitts
- **An oversized white toque** (chef's hat) that's comically too big for his head
- **Rosy pink cheeks**
- **A bulbous pink nose**
- **Big brown dot eyes**

That's it. That's the whole character. The simplicity is the point.

### The Color Palette

- **Skin:** Terracotta/peach — matches ChefFlow's brand color (`#e88f47`)
- **Hat:** Cream/white
- **Cheeks & nose:** Rosy pink
- **Eyes:** Brown dots

He doesn't just live in the app — he _is_ the app's color palette in character form. The cream hat, the peach skin, the rosy cheeks — it's all the same warm palette as the UI.

### The Art Style

Soft edges, hand-painted watercolor textures, no harsh outlines. It feels artisanal — like something a chef would have hand-drawn on a recipe card. This aligns perfectly with ChefFlow's brand: "Ops for Artists." The tool is for creative people. The mascot looks like art, not clip art.

### Gender-Neutral by Design

No gendered features, no gendered clothing, no assumptions. He's a warm little chef blob. Any chef of any background looks at Remy and sees a buddy, not a stereotype. ChefFlow serves every kind of private chef — Remy belongs to all of them.

---

## Why This Design Works

### Instantly Readable

Just like a Minion, you know exactly what he's feeling from across the room. Star eyes = excited. Chin on hand with a question mark = thinking. Straight flat mouth = unimpressed. Tucked into a polka-dot blanket = sleeping. Every emotion reads in a fraction of a second because the design is so stripped down. No distracting details — just shape, eyes, mouth.

### Non-Threatening

This is a business tool that handles real money, real clients, real dietary allergies. The AI advisor could feel intimidating or cold. Instead it's... a little dumpling in a hat too big for his head, waving a heart flag. You trust him immediately. He's not corporate. He's not slick. He's warm.

### Simple Enough to Project Onto

The Minion comparison is deliberate. Minions work because they're simple enough to project onto — you fill in the personality. Remy works the same way, but better because he _does_ have a real personality behind the simple shape.

## The Three Intersections

Remy sits at the intersection of three things that almost never overlap:

1. **Competence** — his personality says "I've been doing this for 40 years, I know what I'm talking about"
2. **Warmth** — his design says "I'm your friend, I'm here to help, I'm not scary"
3. **Whimsy** — his shape says "this is supposed to be fun, this is a creative person's tool"

A serious-looking AI avatar would feel cold. A generic chatbot icon would feel lifeless. A realistic chef character would feel like a specific person. But a little terracotta dumpling in an oversized hat waving a heart flag? That's a companion. That's something you actually want to talk to at 11pm while you're prepping for tomorrow's event.

---

## Why He's the Perfect Mascot for ChefFlow

**"Ops for Artists"** — that's the tagline. ChefFlow is a platform for creative professionals. The mascot needs to feel like art, not enterprise software.

- A serious AI avatar = cold, corporate, intimidating
- A generic chatbot icon = lifeless, forgettable
- A realistic chef character = a specific person, potentially alienating
- **A terracotta potato in an oversized hat** = warm, universal, memorable, fun

He's a companion. A potato with 40 years of Michelin experience, star eyes when you land a big booking, and a polka-dot blanket for when business is slow.
