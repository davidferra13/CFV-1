# ChefFlow Color Identity: Copper and Cast Iron

A kitchen at night. Copper pans hanging on the wall catching warm light. Cast iron on the stove. A dark walnut butcher block. A linen towel.

## The Palette

| Swatch | Name           | Hex       | Feels Like                                                       |
| ------ | -------------- | --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | Burnt Copper   | `#b15c26` | Deep terracotta, the richest expression of the brand             |
| 2      | Golden Amber   | `#eda86b` | The hero color, warm and inviting, the one that catches your eye |
| 3      | Peach Cream    | `#f3c596` | Candlelight on a linen tablecloth                                |
| 4      | Espresso       | `#3e200f` | Dark roasted coffee, a walnut cutting board                      |
| 5      | Charcoal Stone | `#1c1917` | Warm dark gray, like cast iron                                   |
| 6      | Warm Ash       | `#d6d3d1` | Wood ash, linen, the light with a brown undertone                |

## Rules

- These six colors ARE the brand. Everything else (reds, greens, ambers, blues) is functional UI language, not identity.
- The foundation is terracotta on black. Warm copper light on warm dark stone.
- All grays use Tailwind's **stone** scale (brown undertone), never neutral or zinc (cold/blue undertone).
- The palette name is **Copper and Cast Iron**. Use it in design discussions, brand docs, and onboarding.
