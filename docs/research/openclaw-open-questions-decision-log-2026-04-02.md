# OpenClaw Open Questions and Decision Log

> **Date:** 2026-04-02
> **Purpose:** capture the most important unanswered questions that still shape the OpenClaw and ChefFlow build direction.

---

## Top 10 Questions

These are the highest-value still-open or only partially-decided questions to drive next.

1. What is the exact operator workflow when data is wrong: report, override, suppress, or appeal?
   This defines the real correction loop instead of leaving data-quality response half-implicit.

2. What parts of the current app-side enrichment should migrate into OpenClaw first?
   This determines how we reduce architecture drift without breaking working surfaces.

3. What exact data should remain inside OpenClaw, and what should be mirrored into ChefFlow?
   This defines the clean handshake contract between runtime and product.

4. What is the clean handshake contract between OpenClaw and ChefFlow for product detail pages?
   This prevents the website from re-owning runtime facts by accident.

5. What outward-facing claims are allowed without special review, and which require extra review?
   This defines the website presentation boundary and compliance risk posture.

6. What should be shown publicly, what should be chef-only, and what should remain founder-only?
   This decides the real visibility map once the runtime gets stronger.

7. What first-party operator data should we ingest to reduce dependence on scraped sources?
   This is one of the highest-leverage quality and defensibility improvements still open.

8. What are the highest-priority regions after the current anchor footprint?
   This turns the frontier model into an explicit near-term geography plan.

9. What categories of products should be prioritized first?
   This focuses coverage and metadata work on the most valuable product families.

10. What should the founder dashboard show every day to prove the system is progressing?
    This defines the minimum truthful operating surface once the control plane exists.
