# God-Tier Food Operator Survey: Chef OS Standard

Date: 2026-04-02
Status: draft
Purpose: define the zero-compromise product bar for ChefFlow from the perspective of serious food operators
Audience: private chefs first, but valid for caterers, meal prep operators, food trucks, pop-ups, bakeries, and chef-led restaurant operators

---

## What This Survey Is

This is not the same thing as the wave-1 market validation survey.

This is a zero-compromise operator-standard survey. It is intentionally strict. It is designed to answer a narrower question:

If an experienced food operator described the best possible chef operating system they would actually trust, what would that system have to do with no excuses?

This survey is meant for:

- founder-led interviews
- advisory calls with experienced operators
- product-bar setting
- internal pass/fail validation of product direction

This survey is not meant for:

- broad cold distribution
- lightweight demand gen
- generic "feature interest" polling

---

## Website Alignment

This survey should stay aligned with the public ChefFlow promise, not drift into generic restaurant software language.

Use these product truths as the framing:

- ChefFlow is the operating system for private chefs
- ChefFlow also extends to chef-led food operators
- the core promise is clients, events, menus, finances, and staff in one place
- the product should respect craft, privacy, and operator ownership
- direct booking and zero-commission positioning matter
- the system should feel like the anti-marketplace: calmer, clearer, and more transparent

This survey should not reposition the product as:

- a recipe-generation engine
- an AI chef replacement
- a generic SMB software bundle
- a marketplace middleman

---

## AI Policy Alignment

This survey is intentionally constrained by ChefFlow's current AI policy.

Allowed AI territory:

- inbox triage
- thread summaries
- draft follow-ups
- draft proposals and admin copy
- scheduling support
- reminders and next-action surfacing
- structured intake cleanup
- anomaly detection
- privacy-safe operational assistance

Out of scope AI territory:

- recipe creation
- menu invention
- dish ideation
- changing the chef's culinary voice
- autonomous client-facing sends without approval
- silent price setting without explanation
- loose or unclear handling of private data

If an operator wants AI to create recipes or invent dishes, log that as out of scope, not as a product direction change.

## Response Model

Every scored question uses the same standard:

- `Yes` = pass
- `No` = fail
- `Unsure`, `Maybe`, `Nice to have`, or `Only if I still use three other tools` = fail

Survey rule:

- any core-question fail = the God-tier standard is not met
- any applicable branch-question fail = the God-tier standard is not met for that operator type

Use this phrase before the survey starts:

> "Answer this as if you were defining the best operator platform you would actually trust to run your business. If the answer is not a hard yes, treat it as a no."

## Recommended Use Pattern

Use this as a live interview script, not a passive anonymous form.

Recommended respondent profile:

- 8+ years in food operations
- owner, operator, chef-owner, GM, or equivalent
- has handled pricing, client communication, execution, and admin pain directly

Recommended run style:

1. Ask the screener.
2. Ask every core yes/no question straight.
3. Ask exactly one branch.
4. Mark any hesitation as failure, not partial credit.
5. Capture the open follow-up after each section.

---

## What A Perfect Pass Means

If an operator answers `Yes` to every core question and every relevant branch question, the implied product standard is:

- one system for client memory, inquiry handling, proposals, menus, costing, booking, event execution, and close-out
- trustworthy scheduling, capacity control, team coordination, and repeatable SOPs
- defensible compliance, safety, continuity, and dispute records
- strong migration, integration, and data-ownership guarantees
- direct-booking confidence without marketplace dependency
- reliable growth loops for reputation, referrals, and repeat business
- truthful food-cost and margin visibility
- privacy-respecting admin AI without recipe generation
- calmer daily command for both solo chefs and chef-led teams

That is the bar this survey is meant to define.
